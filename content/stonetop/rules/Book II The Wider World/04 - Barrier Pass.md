# Barrier Pass

**Size** *village* (~200 souls)

#### **Population** +0 **Prosperity** +0

- Trade with...
  - ... mammoth herders (ivory, wool, meat)
  - ... other fortress-monasteries

### **Resources**

- Taxes from nearby farm valleys
- Farming (terraced gardens, barley)
- Livestock (goats, sheep, milk, fine wool, meat)
- Timber (from Foothills)
- Parchment, vellum, ink
- Trades: smithing, weaving, tanning, scribing

### **Defenses** +2

- A score of guardians
- A handful of **[[38 - Rime Lords#**Shaksa**|adepts]]**
- A number of **[[38 - Rime Lords#**Major arcana**|tulpas]]**
- Blunt weapons, nets, bolas, bronze shields and mail
- Exceptional fortifications

Five days north of Stonetop via the **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|Highway]]**, after winding through the **[[16 - The Foothills#The Foothills|Foothills]]** and climbing into the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#The Whitefang Mountains|Whitefang Mountains]]**, you'll come to a pass between two rocky slopes. The pass is blocked by a mighty wall, with dwellings and grander structures on the cliffs above.

Long ago, this was the southernmost outpost of the **[[38 - Rime Lords#Rime Lords|Rime Lords]]**. Today, it is home to descendants of their disciples. They are a stoic, unfriendly people who want little to do with those from the south. In Stonetop, they tell tales of folk coming down from Barrier Pass to trade ancient wonders for goats or grain, but none alive remember such a visit.

## **Questions**

- According to the tales, what did the last visitors from Barrier Pass bring to trade, all those years ago?
- When was the last time anyone from Stonetop tried to trade with Barrier Pass? Were they ignored? Chased off? Or what?
- What went wrong when villagers last crossed paths with a party from Barrier Pass up in the Foothills?
- Who do you know (in Gordin's Delve, perhaps) who hails from Barrier Pass or beyond? Why did they leave?
- What do you think lies past that great big wall?
- Who or what do you think they put in those cages that line the Highway as one approaches?
- Why do you think their warriors carry only blunt weapons?

## **Places**

- **The approach**: after winding up switchbacks, the Highway straightens and enters a wide pass with steep rocky slopes on either side.
- **Large cages line the slopes**, swinging in the wind, filled with human (?) bones or starved, raving prisoners.
- **The Wall**, grand and implacable, 50 feet tall and 20 feet wide, spanning the width of the pass.
- **A gate in the Wall**, closed of course, the Highway ending 50 feet before it.
- **The East Slope**, dotted with dwellings, stables, and terraced gardens, all connected with narrow stairs and switch-

- **A waterfall** pours down the east slope, north of the wall. Diverted to gardens and cisterns.
- **The West Slope**, featuring old giantscale structures, now used as dormitories, kitchens, and so forth for monks and guardians.

 **The Great Hall**, its façade elaborately decorated, leading into a vast chamber carved from a mountain cavern. Where residents gather, meditate, train.

## **People**

The people of Barrier Pass descend from the **Rime Lords**' [[38 - Rime Lords|Rime Lords]] mortal disciples. Their names are Nepalese- and Tibetan-inspired (see [[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#1d6 encounter|The Whitefang Mountains]]).

**The Three Honorable Sages** are powerful **[[38 - Rime Lords#**Shaksa**|adepts]]** who preside over Barrier Pass. Each leads one of the three sects.

**The Empty Sky sect** values preser vation and stillness; its monks spend much time in meditative stasis. Led by **Gar Torjeng** (**Instinct** to avoid disruption and distraction).

**The Unmoved Mountain sect** teaches extreme ascetic disciplines, pushing adherents to continually test themselves. Led by **Preshi Kav** (**Instinct** to drive her students towards perfection).

**The White Crystal sect** is devoted to order. The majority of guardians and layfolk follow this sect. Its few monks often lead patrols, mete out justice in nearby farms, and hunt monsters. Led by **Oparma Div** (**Instinct** to tame chaos).

**Monks from each sect** dwell in the fortress, ranging wildly in experience and aptitude. (~40 total, including novices)

The **Guardians** are sworn to defend Barrier Pass. Many are failed monks. They bear ancient bronze shields and armor, but wield no edged weapons. (~20 total)

**The rest of the inhabitants** are lay folk: families and individuals who garden, herd, cook, clean, and otherwise keep the fortress-monastery running. (~140 souls total)

## **Stoic and unfriendly**

Why are the people of Barrier Pass so unwelcoming or even hostile to southerners? That's for you and your table to decide, but perhaps…

- … **Aratis** herself [[03 - Aratis, the Lawkeeper|Aratis, the Lawkeeper]] charged them to preserve the Rime Lords' teachings, and eschew the debased peoples of the south;
- … their ancestors fought off the **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow Builders|Barrow Builders]]** and they still hold a grudge;
- … one of the thirteen surviving **[[38 - Rime Lords#Rime Lords|Rime Lords]]** is hidden in their mountain chambers;
- … a prophecy foretells doom coming from the south;
- … the fortress is tainted by **[[46 - The Things Below#**Hlad, the Devourer, the Eternal Maw**|Y'aaw'kara]]**, and any who dwell here must guard against the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Howling Curse]]**;
- … some combination of the above; or
- … something completely different.

## **TRADE WITH BARRIER PASS**

Surely it would benefit both communities were we to trade with each other!

Requires getting them to talk to you in the first place, which requires 1 of these:

- Bringing them a valuable artifact of the Rime Lords, as a gift or to trade
- A resident in good standing, vouching for you

And then, all of the following:

- Making a compelling offer
- Convincing at least two of the Honored Sages to accept
- Two successful trade missions, each in a different season

When you *mark all the requirements*, increase Fortunes by 1 and add "Trade with Barrier Pass (timber, ivory, parchment, fine wool, goats, sheep)".

Henceforth, when *spring bursts forth and Stonetop has at least 1 Surplus*, Stonetop gains +1 Surplus from trade. Also, when you *Trade & Barter for timber, ivory, fine wool, goats, sheep, parchment, vellum, or ink*, you have advantage.

## **Secrets**

- Secret paths/doors lead in and out of the compound. Outsiders are blindfolded when taken through them, or condemned to death if they learn of their existence.
- There is a hidden vault deep in the mountainside, protected by all manner of magics and **[[38 - Rime Lords#**Major arcana**|tulpas]]**. Only a few inhabitants know it exists; fewer know what it contains.
- One of the monks has started down the path of becoming a **[[38 - Rime Lords#**Instinct** see below|false adept]]**. She is popular and (apparently) quite talented. Only Preshi Kav (Honored Sage of the Unmoved Mountain) suspects anything.

## **Hooks**

- The PCs acquire an artifact of the Rime Lords; maybe they can leverage it into opening trade negotiations?
- A party from Barrier Pass comes to Stonetop, offering ivory/gold/arcana for food/livestock; they are tightlipped, haunted, on edge.
- A condemned criminal escapes from Barrier Pass and seeks refuge; a **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Mountain ape**|shantyara]]** pursues them.
