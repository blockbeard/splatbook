# South Manmarch

If you descend from the **[[42 - The Steplands#The Steplands|Steplands]]** to the southeast, you'll find yourself on a rolling prairie. The ground slopes gently down toward the east, to the banks of the **[[11 - The Dread River#The Dread River|Dread River]]**.

Travelers and merchants aplenty cross these plains. They come north, from **[[29 - Lygos and the South#Lygos and the South|Lygos and the civilized south]]**, heading north to the **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|Highway]]** and from there to **[[32 - Marshedge#Marshedge|Marshedge]]**. Or they head the other way, bearing herbs and textiles, metals and gems, back to the southern cities. Or they come from beyond the Highway, from the **[[34 - North Manmarch#North Manmarch|North Manmarch]]**, to hunt or trade or raid.

But for all those who pass through the South Manmarch, few call it home. Sure, the **[[26 - Hillfolk#Hillfolk|Hillfolk]]** graze their herds here in the warmer months. And some new settlement is always popping up. But the land is dotted with burnt-out homesteads and ruined forts: a mute testament to those who have tried to settle here before. Tried, and failed.

## **Impressions**

### **Always**

- The eternal, unresponsive sky
- Shallow, rocky soil broken by outcrops of white limestone and gray shale
- Smoke from a lone homestead, overlooking a watercourse
- A stream or dry gully, winding lazily down toward the Dread River
- An abandoned, collapsed longhouse
- A Hillfolk horseband, watching from higher ground

### **Spring**

- A mottled carpet of new grass and wildflowers—yellow, pale green, lilac
- Pocket wetlands, home to nesting ducks recently returned from the south
- A great cloud of birds, bursting forth from the brush

### **Summer**

- Squinting into a blinding sky
- The fervid and unrelenting chorus of summer insects, shifting in tone and rhythm with time of day
- Tallgrass in full flush, waist-high and rippling in a southerly wind.
- A mob of white-tailed deer or mighty elk, grazing on a long slope

### **Autumn**

- Grassheads gone rust-colored, stalks still green
- A streamside stand of cottonwoods, glowing gold in the dusk light

### **Winter**

- Days of scouring, southwesterly wind
- Powdery snowdrifts, stretching leeward from ripples in the landscape
- Patches of dead grass, showing orange through the snow

## **A long-disputed land**

Before the **[[46 - The Things Below#The Time of Cataclysm|Cataclysm]]** the Dread River formed the eastern border of **[[43 - Stone Lords#Stone Lords|Stone Lord]]** territory. After the Stone Lords fell, the **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow Builders|Barrow Builders]]** fought each other back and forth across this expanse, grinding themselves down, like shards of shale abraded to dust over time.

Now, the South Manmarch is the border between the civilized south and the hinterlands of the World's End. It's a lawless place, full of dangerous beasts and even more dangerous people. Nomadic Hillfolk claim much of the region, and **[[40 - South Manmarch#Bandit|bandits]]** are a constant threat.

And yet, the lure of the prairie is great. Every year or so, someone tries to carve out a piece of for themselves. A new settlement might be:

- A family from Marshedge or the North Manmarch, starting their own farm
- An ambitious Lygosi merchant, investing in a new trading post or caravansary
- Some would-be chief or warlord from the **[[34 - North Manmarch#The Manmarchers|Manmarchers]]**, building a new hamlet or hillfort
- The **conquering armies** of the South [[29 - Lygos and the South#Lygos and the South|Lygos and the South]], establishing a foothold

Such settlements rarely last. They fall prey to natural disaster or roving beasts, to rivals or bandits, or to the certain wrath of the Hillfolk who claim these lands as theirs.

Or perhaps, if you believe the stories, there's something darker afoot. Perhaps these lands really are haunted. Haunted by the numerous souls who've died here. Or by foul things, slithering out of the Dread River. Perhaps the land is cursed, and no settlement can ever survive. Perhaps.

## **The Flintwash**

Draining out of the Steplands and into the Dread River, this watercourse draws thickets, copses of cottonwood, wildlife, and wanderers to its gentle banks. The Hillfolk call it *Maengwalc* in their own tongue—"Flintwash," after the stony plateau from which its headwaters spring.

Hundreds of rills and brooks feed the Flintwash, and in its lower reaches, it's quite broad and difficult to cross. Yet ways do exist. How might a traveler get from one side to the other? Ask a PC who would know, but perhaps there is…

- … an old bridge from the time of the **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]**;
- … a newer bridge, built at great expense by some southern merchant or noble;
- … a shallow ford; wide, rocky and usually safe;
- … a ferry, run by enterprising souls;
- … some combination of the above; or
- … something else entirely.

## **Questions**

- Who did you know who tried to settle in the South Manmarch? What fate befell them?
- What bandit crew is currently the most feared? Who leads them, and what makes them so dangerous?
- At what landmark do merchant caravans typically encamp? Why do they stop there?
- What landmark does everyone who travels these lands know to avoid? Why?

## **Hooks**

- Someone has seized the Flintwash crossing, and is charging exorbitant tolls
- An ambitious chief from the North Manmarch is sending settlers south of the Highway
- The PCs eagerly await a caravan, but it gets waylaid by bandits
- A ruin in the region becomes a place of pilgrimage, drawing the faithful of Helior/Aratis from all over

For any one of these, pick 1 or have someone roll the Die of Fate.

- 5 A place sacred to the **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the wild|spirits of the wild]]**

### **1d12** battlefield or remains

- 1 Truly ancient: from the time of the **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]**
- 2 From the **[[55 - Appendix A - Ages of the World#Time of Cataclysm|Time of Cataclysm]]**, when humanity rebelled and woke the Things Below

## **Dangers**

### **Hazards**

- Baking sun, no shade for miles
- Nasty turn in the weather: lashing wind, bitter cold, blistering heat, sleet/hail/rain/snow; lightning
- Flash flooding in valleys/on slopes
- Lack of shelter, water, or wood for a long-burning fire (try dried aurochs dung, if you can find it)
- Hidden holes/burrows, twisting an ankle or worse
- **[[14 - The Flats#Prairie fire|Prairie fire]]**

### **Monsters**

- Predators: **[[23 - The Great Wood#Cougar|cougars]]**, **[[42 - The Steplands#Kleztigr|kleztigr]]**, **[[14 - The Flats#Pack drake|pack drakes]]**, or **[[23 - The Great Wood#Nailadd|wolves]]**
- Big ornery beasts: **[[14 - The Flats#Aurochs|aurochs]]**, **[[23 - The Great Wood#Boar|boar]]**, or a **[[14 - The Flats#Thunder drake|thunder drake]]**
- **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the wild|Spirits of the wild]]**
- **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Ghosts|Ghosts]]** or **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Revenants|revenants]]**, lurking near ancient battlefields or ruined settlements

### **People**

- **[[26 - Hillfolk#Hillfolk|Hillfolk]]**, if they're of a mind to do violence
- **[[34 - North Manmarch#North Manmarch|North Manmarchers]]**, heading south or defending a new settlement
- Merchants from **[[32 - Marshedge#Marshedge|Marshedge]]**, heading south or coming home
- Southern **[[29 - Lygos and the South#Merchants|merchants]]** and their caravan guards, plus those traveling with them, like adventurers or an **[[29 - Lygos and the South#Antiquarian|antiquarian]]**
- **[[29 - Lygos and the South#Assassin|Armies of the south]]**, coming north to exact vengeance, secure a resource, establish a foothold

> [!monster] ## **Horned drake**
>
> *Solitary*, *huge*, *hardy*
>
> **HP** 24; **Armor** 3 (stony hide)
>
> **Damage** trampling charge d10+5 (*hand*, *area*, *forceful*) or horn gore d10+5 (*close*, *forceful*, *messy*, 1 piercing)
>
> **Instinct** to protect their young
>
> - Issue a honking call
> - Stand its ground, still as a rock
> - Lurch suddenly into a charge
> - Absorb raw elemental power (regaining 1d8 HP)
>
> The behemoth of the southern grasslands is striking for its great size, the quartz-like plates of its segmented hide, and its fearsome horns. The horns vary greatly from beast to beast, ranging from a single spike on its snout or forehead to a rack of five or six curved points edging the flanges of its massive skull. An intimidating beast, even at rest.
>
> To the Hillfolk, the downing of a *d'lkornek* ("thorn-dragon") is a hero's feat, to be followed by a week of harvest and celebration at the kill site.
>
> **Something interesting**: Some scholars hold that the Stone Lords imbued horned drakes with elemental qualities. Specimens of their hide are commonly mistaken for dolostone or some other mineral, and Hillfolk prize their horns (Value 3).
>
> **Something useful:** The horns of a *d'lkornek* act like **[[43 - Stone Lords#Power-crystal|power-crystals]]**, but larger and with great capacity. They are extremely dangerous to harvest; a wise butcher slowly bleeds away their energy over the course of days.
>
> Adult horned drakes might be accompanied by their young (*group*, *large*, *hardy*; **HP** 14; **Damage** d8+3; **Instinct** to get curious, to play fight), whom they aggressively defend.
