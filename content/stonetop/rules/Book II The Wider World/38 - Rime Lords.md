# Rime Lords

In the utmost north, the Rime Lords made their domain. They spread through the Whitefang Mountains [[54 - The Whitefang Mountains|The Whitefang Mountains]] and the tundra beyond, embracing the ice and the cold and the terrible howling emptiness.

The Rime Lords developed all manner of ascetic disciplines and esoteric arts. They inured their bodies to the cold. They sent their minds wandering through spirit realms. They shaped matter and energy with their will, imposed stillness on the world, froze pockets of reality in timeless stasis.

Disciples came from the farm-valleys and the mammoth herders, to study at the feet of these living gods. Their teachings were harsh, rigorous, and demanding. But for a time, monasteries dotted the north and the ways of the Rime Lords flourished.

But then the servants of the Stone Lords [[43 - Stone Lords|Stone Lords]] rebelled and set loose the Things Below [[46 - The Things Below|The Things Below]]. Something cracked open at the very roof of the world, and the voice of Y'aaw'kara (the Howling Wind, the Flayer of Flesh) was unleashed. Bloodthirst and madness spread.

Some Rime Lords fell to the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#Ice drake|Howling Curse]]**. Others were brought low by mortal mobs run rampant. The thirteen survivors agreed that something must be done. They each retreated to a place of power, sent forth their minds, and together sealed the rift beneath a glacier of **[[38 - Rime Lords#Dark ice|dark ice]]**, quieting Y'aaw'kara's voice.

The Howling Curse dulled to a constant whisper. But the last thirteen Rime Lords had sacrificed themselves. Their bodies remain encased in ice, their minds working the magic intended to keep Y'aaw'kara's voice at bay for eternity.

## **Names**

Sanskrit-inspired: Akativet, Artarapishi, Dahishika, Goranat, Kanishanu, Mamaranu, Marash, Nilani, Prayanavi, Ralananan, Ranaji, Shayand, Supnam, Vitatana

Often combined with a title of "Gar" ("teacher") or appellations of "Div" ("the holy") or Kav ("the wise"). For example "Gar Akativet," "Kanishanu Div," or "Ranaji Kav."

### **Thoughtform**

Objects, sensations, or vignettes of manifest will, formed of base matter or energy, like how a spirit manifests a body. Not real, but real enough.

- A grand tree, growing in a lightless cave. Its bark and wood are shaped of stone, but its petals/leaves/fruits crumble to dust and water when shed.
- A bubble of stillness on a wind-swept summit, boasting an elaborately decorated altar. The altar is flimsy as frost, dissolving if touched.
- A chant that fills a valley, echoing endlessly from the mountainsides.
- A spring in a low valley, the water never freezing. Drinking vessels are found at the water's edge, impossibly beautiful, but melting into water if removed.

## **Dark ice**

Purplish ice, which stays cold and frozen except in the hottest furnace. The Rime Lords could form and shape it at will. Some of their disciples learned the trick, too, but the art has largely been lost.

Pick or roll for form and characteristic.

### **1d12** form

- 1-3 Fragment (chip/shard/chunk/etc.), from something larger
- 4-5 Enigmatic shape (cube/sphere/spiral/etc.), its purpose unclear
- 6-7 Mundane object (tool/utensil/container/etc.), though perhaps not easily recognized as such
- 8-9 Art or decoration (jewel/statue/tiles/torc/amulet/ring/etc.)
- 10-12 Construction (door/stairway/arch/bricks/walls/etc.), likely *immobile*

Consider rolling 1d6 to inform size/scale.

### **1d12** characteristic

- 1 Breathtakingly beautiful
- 2 Dims light/color in its presence
- 3 Absorbs/baffles sound
- 4 Exceptionally hard, roll 1d6 for degree (otherwise, hard as mundane ice)
- 5 Presents a tangible barrier to spirits, even when unmanifest
- 6 Pieces persist when removed, instead of melting like normal ice
- 7-8 Roll 1d6 twice and combine
- 9-12 Just (just) a bit of never-melting ice

Consider rolling 1d6 to inform how pristine the dark ice is.

### **A column of ice**

*magical*, *immobile*

Amidst a high, lonely meadow stands a pillar of dark purple ice, 12 feet tall and 2 feet thick. It rests on a circular slab of rune-etched stone, a dozen paces across, untouched by time or nature. There's a stillness to the place, a tension in the air.

**Something interesting:** The runes appear to be some sort of spell, waiting to be triggered; it'd take days of study to puzzle out the command word (and you risk triggering it accidentally).

**Something useful:** You can determine the command word with a few hours of study (less if you're versed in Rime Lord script).

When you *speak the command word near the column*, the dark ice expands in a flash, encasing the stone slab and anything on it. The ice traps even spirits (manifest or not). Mortal prisoners soon perish, their souls ensnared. The dark ice never melts and resists all but the mightiest magic and blows.

### **A dark ice knife**

*hand*, *magical*, *crude*

A hilt of bone, with a spike of dark ice tapering to a sharp point. The ice is hard as stone, and not especially sharp.

When you *stab an undead entity with the knife*, ask the GM whether it is driven by obsession, hunger, and/or vengeance. If the answer is "yes," then deal +1d4 damage (ignores armor), and leave the entity briefly disoriented. Should this reduce the entity to 0 HP, the dark ice knife melts away along with whatever bound the entity to this world.

### **A small black sphere**

*magical*, *indestructible*, *immobile*

Found in or near some Rime Lord ruin, this… thing… appears to be a perfect sphere of lead, maybe 2 inches across. It hovers motionless in the air. No amount of physical force can mar it, nor move it from its relative position.

When you *focus all your will upon the black sphere*, roll +WIS: **on a 10+**, you can slowly move the sphere with your mind, so long as you keep your attention fixed upon the task; **on a 7-9**, pick 1:

- Mark a debility and move the sphere as per a 10+
- Move the sphere, but only a few inches

## **Tulpa**

A tulpa is a constructed **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the wild|spirit]]**, created by the powerful will of another. The Rime Lords crafted many such entities, which linger on in their creators' wake.

A tulpa has the *spirit* and *construct* tags, with a move that reflects them manifesting or possessing a physical form. They are animated by more than biology (+4 HP) and usually lack vital organs (+3 HP, +1 Armor). Pick or roll a purpose, size, ability, tether, and disposition. Use these elements to inform how it manifests and behaves.

### **1d12** purpose

- 1-2 Battle/sparring/protection
- 3-4 Companionship/advice/diplomacy/subterfuge
- 5-6 Labor/mundane tasks/convenience
- 7-8 Curiosity/instruction/assistance/practice
- 9-10 Something obscure, or very specific
- 11-12 Roll 1d10 twice, combine

### **1d12** size

- 2-3 Like a human child (*small*)
- 4-7 Human-sized
- 8-10 Like a horse, a cart, etc. (*large*)
- 11-12 Like an elephant, or bigger (*huge*)

### **1d12** ability

- 2 Emotional influence/control
- 3 Elemental manipulation (ice/wind/water/etc.)

- 5 Invisibility/stealth/observation/tracking

- 8 Illusions/false senses/thoughtforms
- 9 Strength/speed/prowess/mobility
- 10 Dangerous physical attack(s)
- 11-12 Roll 1d10 twice, combine

- 3-4 Small, portable object: unable to manipulate it or leave its presence
- 5-6 Sculpture or construct: can animate it, but cannot leave its presence
- 7-8 Building or complex: can freely move about within, but cannot leave
- 9-10 Place or object (roll 1d6 for size/scale): can travel but must periodically return
- 11 Living creature/person: can leave their presence for short periods
- 12 Untethered: free-roaming, independent

### **1d12** disposition

- 1 Friendly/polite/helpful

- 3 Curious/playful/fickle
- 4 Resentful/duty-bound/compelled
- 5 Proud/dedicated/honorable
- 6 Confused/erratic/distraught
- 7 Boastful/aggressive/hostile
- 8 Malicious/cruel/devious
- 9 Timid/shy/meek
- 10 Focused/obsessive/anxious

The Rime Lords taught that the self could be perfected. That the needs and frailties of the body could be purged. That the mind could free itself of the flesh and know the unseen world of spirits and fundamental forces. That the will could conquer all. The goal was nothing short of immortality, the preservation of the perfect self.

For most of the Rime Lords' mortal disciples, these teachings did little more than strengthen the body and clear the mind. But for some—the adepts—these teachings unlocked a fragment of their masters' power.

Rivalries were fierce among the adepts even when the Rime Lords walked the land. When the last Rime Lords disappeared, these rivalries flourished into schisms, sects, and violent purges. Some of the adepts fell. Others went into hiding. Still others passed their knowledge on, building schools and monasteries around them.

To create an adept, start with the stats above. Pick or roll for an instinct and 1-4 moves to represent their capabilities.

- 7-8 To prove their superiority
- 9-10 To tame chaos
- 11-12 To test themselves or their students
