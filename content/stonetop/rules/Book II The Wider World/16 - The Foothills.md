# The Foothills

Two days northwest of Stonetop, the rolling **[[14 - The Flats#The Flats|Flats]]** give way to hills, climbing up into the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#The Whitefang Mountains|Whitefang Mountains]]**). The **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|Highway]]** cuts through these hills, leading to **[[04 - Barrier Pass#Barrier Pass|Barrier Pass]]**. To the east, the hills wrap around the **[[23 - The Great Wood#The Great Wood|Great Wood]]**.

The Foothills are rugged and forest-strewn, crisscrossed with streams, dotted with gorges and ponds and small lakes. In ages past, it was a borderland between the **[[38 - Rime Lords#Rime Lords|Rime Lords]]** and the **[[24 - Green Lords#Green Lords|Green Lords]]**, and later it was the extent of the **Barrow Builders'** reach [[05 - Barrow Builders|Barrow Builders]]. Nowadays, it is mostly uninhabited, but both Stonetop and Barrier Pass come here to harvest timber.

## **Impressions**

#### **Always**

- Hills rising up from the Flats, mountains looming behind them
- Young thin trees on the southernmost slopes, getting bigger and older as you press into the hills
- Echoes of birdsong, beast-bellows, tumbling rocks, raised voices

#### **Spring**

- Thin branches drooping with fresh green leaves
- Mud slipping and squishing underfoot
- Surprising chill of shadowed vales, where snow still lingers

#### **Summer**

- Muggy, humid heat
- Sweat stinging your eyes as you hump up another slope
- Cold clear water from a gurgling stream, tasting better than anything

#### **Autumn**

- Slopes turned red and orange and yellow, then black and brown and gray
- Plump squirrels and other critters, busily foraging for winter
- Cold, clammy mist lasting well into the day

### **Winter**

- Waist-deep snow drifts in the valleys
- Frozen-over streams, water gurgling ominously from below
- Squeak of trees, swaying in the cold wind
- Short, magical twilight as the sun drops below the mountains

#### **PERMANENT LOGGING CAMP**

Suppose we built a few cabins and kept loggers up there year-round?

Requires all of the following:

- Two or three permanent dwellings, just off the Highway
- Supplies, furnishings, and tools for those dwellings (Value 2)
- 5-6 capable adults, willing and able to relocate and log year-round
- An extra wagon (Value 3) and extra horse or mule (Value 3) to haul timber to and from Stonetop
- Four seasons of operation, each generating a wagonload of timber (Value 2)

When you *mark all the requirements*, add "Logging: timber, wood" to the resources list and increase Fortunes by 1.

Henceforth, as long as the camp is in operation, the steading has a ready supply of timber for improvements and other projects, and it consumes 1 less Surplus than usual in winter. However, the logging camp consumes 1 Surplus every summer or else it ceases operation.

## **Hazards**

- Getting lost among the hills and trees
- Treacherous terrain (loose footing, scree, slippery slopes, potholes, deadfall, etc.)
- Flash flooding, especially in ravines and valleys
- Harsh weather, especially rain and snow
