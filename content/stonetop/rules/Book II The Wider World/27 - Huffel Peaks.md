# Huffel Peaks

This range runs along the western edge of the **[[14 - The Flats#The Flats|Flats]]** and the **[[42 - The Steplands#The Steplands|Steplands]]**, connecting with the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#The Whitefang Mountains|Whitefang Mountains]]** in the north. They're not as tall and menacing as the Whitefangs, but they are jagged things of granite and basalt, home to a number of volcanoes, and rich in minerals and ore.

## **Impressions**

### **Always**

- Slabs of jagged stone: impassive, looming, somehow still surprising
- Tall thin trees (aspen, pines, firs) in the lower slopes and valleys; windswept junipers higher up
- The stone-on-stone clink and thunk as rocks tumble down a slope
- A sense of being surrounded, cut off

### **Spring**

- Snow clinging to slopes and peaks, slowly melting
- Pale green on the tips of branches; meadows bursting with wildflowers

### **Summer**

- Too-bright sky as you step out of the shade
- Lips cracking in a hot, dry breeze
- Hot sun beating down on you while, somehow, you're getting rained on

### **Autumn**

- Slopes and meadows of yellow and gold
- A bitter chill in the now-damp air
- Air pressure dropping as a storm rolls in

### **Winter**

- Black stone peeking out from white
- Trees drooping under snow
- A sense of stillness, despite the constant wind

## **The World's End**

Beyond the Huffel Peaks lies a vast expanse of water. But what lies between the mountains and the sea? That's for you and your group to decide, and the answer might vary depending on which direction you go through the mountains. But perhaps one might find...

- … mighty cliffs, pounded by relentless waves;
- … a gentle slope, a bountiful lowland, vast sandy beaches, a mild clime;
- … a chain of islands, some tiny, some quite large;
- … a wine-dark sea, the civilized lands of the south to be found at its farthest shores;
- … deserted ruins of the **[[45 - Tempest Lords#Tempest Lords|Tempest Lords]]**, the **[[18 - Forge Lords#Forge Lords|Forge Lords]]** or both;
- … the **[[51 - Ustrina#Ustrina|Ustrina]]**, in numbers far greater than anyone would suspect;
- … an isolated people, their ancestors a mix of refugees from when the **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]** fell;
- … colonies from the civilized lands of the **[[29 - Lygos and the South#Lygos and the South|South]]**, eking out a living;
- … some combination of the above; or
- … something else entirely.

## **Questions**

- Who did you once meet who claimed to have reached the far side of the Huffel Peaks? Do you believe their stories about an endless body of water?
- What legendary treasure is said to be hidden in those peaks?
- How long has it been since one of the volcanoes erupted violently? How did it affect Stonetop/Gordin's Delve/the Hillfolk?
- You don't really think that there are dragons in the Huffel Peaks, do you?

## **Dangers**

### **Hazards**

- Getting lost (maze of mountains, canyons, passes, and valleys)
- Sheer rock face to climb, and a reason to climb it
- Steep slopes, unstable footing (narrow paths, wet stone, scree, thin waterlogged soil, mud, crumbling rock)
- Flash flooding in valleys/on slopes
- An unexpected obstacle/hazard: go forward and risk it, or backtrack and lose half a day?
- **[[42 - The Steplands#Mudslide|Mudslides]]** and **[[42 - The Steplands#Rockslide|rockslides]]**
- **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#Altitude sickness|Altitude sickness]]**, especially near the peaks

### **Harsh mountain weather**

- Baking sun, dry air, lack of water
- Fog banks/low clouds, blocking vision and soaking everything
- Buffeting wind, drowning out voices and keeping you off-balance
- Powerful gusts, threatening to toss you aside
- Rain and wind rolling up from below
- Rain and sleet pelting you, soaking you, threatening to wash you away
- Thunder or lightning: making you flinch/jerk/jolt, triggering rockslides
- Brutal cold and ceaseless wind, leading to **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#Hypothermia & frostbite|hypothermia & frostbite]]**
- Snow and ice burying a pass or trail, making narrow paths impossible
- Snow and ice building up, forming an **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#Avalanche|avalanche]]**

### **Volcanic activity**

- Superheated air (d4 damage) or poisonous vapors (d4 damage, ignores armor), especially in enclosed spaces
- Searing hot stones (d6 damage)
- Steam vents/geysers (d8 damage, may be *forceful*)
- Scalding hot mud/water (d8 damage, ignores armor)
- Lava flows (d10+2 damage, *grabby*, *messy*, 3 piercing)

### **Earthquake**

- The ground shakes briefly, alarming but not dangerous
- Powerful tremors make footing unstable, knock rocks loose, cause animals to panic
- Tremors persist: people lose their footing, large rocks tumble (d8 damage, *forceful*), trails and paths are damaged
- It's a big one: **mudslides** and **[[42 - The Steplands#Rockslide|rockslides]]**, **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#Avalanche|avalanches]]**, whole rockfaces come tumbling down

> [!monster] ## **Caralandrao**
>
>  *Horde*, *small*, *stealthy*, *hoarder*
>
> **HP** 3; **Armor** 2 (shell)
>
> **Damage** pincers d4 with disadvantage (*hand*, *grabby*)
>
> **Special qualities** spider-climb, camouflaged shell
>
> **Instinct** to feed on magic
>
> - Sense mystic emanations from afar
> - Get close without being noticed
> - Skitter-skatter, dart and weave
> - Make off with something magical
>
> Are they crabs? Spiders? Hard to say. Whatever they are, they've got too many too-long limbs, shells that blend in with the rocky terrain, and an appetite for magical artifacts. They'll drag their prizes back to their nests to feed. They can suck the power right out of minor trinkets, but powerful arcana can feed a small colony indefinitely.
>
> And yet, they keep venturing out to find more.

> [!monster] ## **Fire drake**
>
>  *Group*, *aggressive*, *tough*
>
> **HP** 10; **Armor** 2 (scales)
>
> **Damage** bite d8 (*hand*, *grabby*)
>
>
> **Special qualities** fireproof; caustic, burning blood
>
> **Instinct** to get riled up easily
>
> - Sound off in a deep, threatening chorus
> - Blast a jet of burning, caustic blood from its eyes, d8 damage (*near*, *messy*, *reload*, ignores armor), but lose 1d4 HP
> - Shake and worry and toss aside a foe it has bitten, d8+2 damage (*hand*, *forceful*)
>
> Picture a horned lizard, but big as a large boar and with a mouth full of short jagged teeth. They're often encountered basking near hot springs or mudpots or other geothermal vents, soaking up the heat until they swell and glow like embers. Of course, once they notice you, they stop basking and start attacking.
>
> **Something interesting:** Fire drake blood is caustic and catches fire when exposed to air; a jar of it deals d6 damage (*thrown*, *area*, *dangerous*, ignores armor, Value 1), but good luck harvesting it.
>
> **Something useful:** The trick is to tap a vein with a metal tube, and drain the blood directly into a wax-sealed jar. Just… be careful.
