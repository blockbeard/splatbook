# The Flats

Stonetop sits on the easternmost edge of the **Flats**, a rolling tallgrass prairie that stretches all the way to the **[[27 - Huffel Peaks#Huffel Peaks|Huffel Peaks]]**, the **[[16 - The Foothills#The Foothills|Foothills]]** and the **[[42 - The Steplands#Spring|Steplands]]**.

The prairie is dotted with small hills, outcrops, and the occasional copse of trees huddled against the ceaseless wind. Winding streams cut through the soil. But mostly? Just mile after mile after mile of grass.

# **Impressions**

# **Always**

- A southerly wind, ever present
- Mountains looming on the far horizon
- A sky so big it beggars the soul
- Birds of prey, or carrion eaters, floating high above
- Clouds, always in motion: high and wispy, or big and fluffy, or massive and dark like an anvil ready to drop

### **Spring**

- Patchworks of hopeful green, pale white, dry yellow
- Carpet of last year's grass, folded flat by wind and heavy snow
- Soft crunch and pop underfoot
- A damp chill in the wind, smell of rain and wet soil
- Chirp chirp chirp, warble

### **Summer**

- Green and yellow stems topped with a riot of flowers, rippling and bobbing in the wind
- The itch and scratch of nettles, thistle, and stiff sandpaper stalks
- Constant noise: birdsong, insect-buzz, rustling grass
- Relentless sun, unbroken by shade
- A wall of rain, rolling towards you

# **Autumn**

- Crinkle, snap, and rustle with every step, every movement
- Stiff stalks, gold in the low sun, nodding in the breeze
- Lonely chitter of birds, unseen
- Morning frost, or thick blankets of fog

## **Winter**

- Snow billowing in the wind, obscuring everything
- A white expanse, still as death, poked through with grassy stubble
- The cold, wet, exhausting trudge through waist-high drifts
- The squeak of snow underfoot on a bitterly cold day

### **The Gwead**

Villagers call the Flats-grasses **the Gwead** ("the Weave" or "the Weaving"). It's a bewildering array of species. Some are short and wispy, others thick and taller than a tall man. They bloom in a myriad of shapes and colors, and sport every manner of prickle, thorn, and seedpod.

The Gwead will not be tamed. Burn the grasses, plow the soil, tear things up by their roots—it doesn't matter. The Gwead always come back, thicker and hardier than before. And sometimes, it seems, the grasslands take their revenge.

- When was the last time anyone tried to plant crops beyond the Old Wall?
- What uncanny fate befell them?

Why can crops be managed within the **[[02 - The Village of Stonetop#**The village of Stonetop**|Old Wall]]**, but not beyond? That's for you to decide. Maybe…

- … magic tied to the **[[02 - The Village of Stonetop#**The village of Stonetop**|Stone]]** makes the land more pliable, easier to tame;
- … something buried beneath the Old Wall keeps the Gwead at bay;
- … something about the soil keeps the Gwead from taking root; or
- … whoever built the ruins on which Stonetop was founded, they made a pact with the spirits of the Gwead.

**Prairie fire**

 The Gwead is prone to burning, sparked by lightning, an errant flame, or (if you believe the tales) Hillfolk intentionally setting the Flats ablaze. Early spring, late summer, and early autumn are particularly prone, especially after a few weeks without rain.

**Instinct** to run unchecked across the land

- <sup>ä</sup> Start with just a few crackling flames, a column of smoke
- <sup>ä</sup> Grow implacably, creeping outward
- <sup>ä</sup> Smother the landscape in choking smoke (debilities)
- <sup>ä</sup> Hit them with a wave of heat (d4 damage)
- <sup>ä</sup> Linger and burn for minutes or hours (d6 damage, messy)
- <sup>ä</sup> Tear a crimson line, faster than you can sprint, bonfire-hot (d10 damage, ignores armor, messy)
- <sup>ä</sup> Run out of fuel, or get smothered by rain

**Something interesting**: To really spread and burn hot, a prairie fire needs years of built-up fuel, dry conditions, and steady wind; even then, it can only burn at its hottest for a short period of time.

## **Aurochs**

*Group*, *large*, *ornery*, *powerful* **HP** 14, **Armor** 2 (thick hide, tough) **Damage** gore d8+5 (*close*, *forceful*, *messy*, 1 piercing), kick/trample d8+3 (*hand*, *forceful*)

**Instinct** to spook or to get angry, in equal measure

- ä Send an attacker flying
- ä Stampede (*area*, *forceful*, *messy*)
- ä Wheel suddenly, with surprising grace and speed
- ä Keep going past when it should be dead or dying

Big, powerful, wild cattle with horns longer than your arm and thicker than your hand can grip around. Even the does have big horns, and aren't shy about using them.

They winter in the Steplands, in small bands of 3-5 adults. In spring, though, they come down to the Flats, form up into herds of 30 or more, and graze and graze and graze. They'll break up into smaller bands by midsummer, then head back into the hills before first snow.

The Hillfolk hunt them, riding at the flanks of a herd and picking off one or two with arrows, keeping their damned distance. You do not want to get too close to one of these things, especially not after you wound it.

A full-grown aurochs carcass (*immobile*), properly butchered and transported back to town, counts as 1 Surplus. A pair of aurochs horns is Value 1.

## **Bandit**

 *Group*, *stealthy*, *cautious*, *devious* **HP** 6; **Armor** up to 2 (hides, shields) **Damage** spears d8 (*close*, *thrown*) or slings d6 (*near*)

**Instinct** to survive at the expense of others

- ä Prepare a deception, to lure travelers from the roads
- ä Spring an ambush when their prey takes the bait
- ä Take captives or slit throats, so that no one escapes to tell tales
- ä Flee into the grass if things go south

The magic of the Roads and the open terrain make a life of banditry far from easy, but somehow, they crop up every few years. Might be Hillfolk, disgraced or desperate, but more often than not, they're scoundrels who'd rather bleed their fellows than scrape by up in Gordin's Delve.

## **Beznpol**

*Solitary*, *cautious*, *fierce*

**HP** 12; **Armor** 3

**Damage** burrowing talons d10 (*hand*), needle teeth d8 (*hand*, *messy*, 3 piercing) **Special Qualities** acid-proof

**Instinct** to constantly search for food

- <sup>ä</sup> Dig up serious quantities of soil
- <sup>ä</sup> Bound out of the grass and onto prey
- <sup>ä</sup> Spray a stupefying musk (*reach*, *area*, debilities)
- ä Curl up into a ball of hard, jagged scales (4 armor)

Great big armored mustelids, like a cross between a stoat, a badger, and a pangolin, but like 6 feet long not counting the tail. They roam the Flats digging up burrows of just about anything, but they especially love to stir up a **[[14 - The Flats#Hlomren|hlomren]]** nest and chomp chomp chomp. They're far from the top of the food chain, though. If startled, they'll spray musk and attempt to flee. If cornered, they'll curl up and hope the attacker grows bored.

A skilled tanner can turn a pair of hides into a magnificent beznpol scale coat (2 armor, *warm*, protects against acid, Value 3). Hillfolk champions are known to wear them.

## **Frythanc**

 *Solitary*, *large*, *stealthy*, *terrifying*

**HP** 16; **Armor** 0

**Damage** swooping talons d10+2 (*hand*, *close*, *forceful*, *grabby*), beak d10 (*reach*, disadvantage)

**Special Qualities** incredible eyesight, terrible hearing

**Instinct** to wait for the right moment

- ä Soar on thermals, thousands of feet up
- ä Swoop down like a thunderbolt, snatching up prey
- ä Drag prey up to 30 or 40 feet with slow, mighty flaps—then drop them

Great scaly birds, wings like 40 feet across, sky blue and white feathers on their undersides to hide in the sky. They're said to roost in the Huffel Peaks but hunt the Flats, snatching up the unwary—man, woman, deer, horse—and dropping them to the ground. The drop usually kills or cripples its prey, and then it alights to dine at its leisure.

They are, thank Tor, very rare, with only one mated pair hunting the Flats at a time. On the other side of the Huffel Peaks, though… who knows?

## **Hlomren**

*Horde*, *tiny*, *organized*

**HP** 1; **Armor** 3 (carapace, size)

**Damage** acid-dripping mandibles d6-2 (*hand*, *messy*, 3 piercing), acid glob d6-2 (*reach*, *messy*, *reload*, 1 piercing)

**Instinct** to protect the colony

- <sup>ä</sup> Burrow into/out of/through loose soil
- <sup>ä</sup> Swarm over a threat
- <sup>ä</sup> Burst with acid when killed, d4 damage (*close*, *area*)

These red-shelled insects look like a cross between an ant and a beetle, but the size of a small cat. They form colonies a few-hundred large, typically on higher ground. A colony will harvest all the dead grass in a few miles, hibernate through winter, and then relocate in spring to a part of the Flats that hasn't burned or been harvested in a few years. They mostly ignore people and other large animals, but they'll swarm if threatened.

#### **Shellback drake**

*Solitary*, *cautious*, *hardy*, *patient*

**HP** 16; **Armor** 3 (carapace, scales)

**Damage** snapping, bone-crushing bite d10 (*hand*, *close*, *grabby*, *messy*, 1 piercing)

**Special Qualities** nearly blind,

senses vibrations

**Instinct** to lash out at "food" in range

- <sup>ä</sup> Burrow into soil
- <sup>ä</sup> Wait for prey half-buried

Like a snapping turtle, but 6 feet long and 400 pounds, with big, badger-like claws. Way faster than you think. It'll dig itself under a layer of soil, and wait long enough for the gwead to start growing over it. Then, when something comes near? SNAP!

An intact shell is worth quite a bit (*immobile*, Value 2). But good luck killing one without cracking the shell.

## **Thunder drake**

 *Solitary*, *large*

**HP** 20; **Armor** 3 (scales, crest)

**Damage** gore, trample, swat d10+3 (*hand*, *close*, *forceful*, *messy*, 1 piercing)

**Special Qualities** lightning-proof,

hard of hearing

**Instinct** to protect its turf

- ä Bellow (*near*, *area*, *loud*, *forceful*, *reload*)
- ä Burrow and gnaw through rock
- ä Sometimes: belch forth a bolt of lightning, d10+3 damage (*near*, *forceful*, *loud*, ignores armor)

Sometimes, out on the Flats, you'll see what looks to be small hills in the distance. I advise you to steer clear. They probably aren't hills, really, but huge piles of dirt that a thunder drake has moved as it digs. And hoo boy, you do not want to stumble into one of them on their home turf.

**Something interesting:** Thunder drakes eat mostly grass and shrubs, but they have a taste for certain ores. They spend their time grazing, sleeping, and digging up rocks.

**Something useful:** They particularly savor the taste of copper, and relish **[[45 - Tempest Lords#**Aetherium**|aetherium]]**. If they find a source of it, they'll chew it right up and spend the next few days belching lightning bolts.

When you *are near a thunder drake when it bellows*, roll +CON: **on a 10+**, pick 1; **on a 7-9,** pick 2; **on a 6-**, all 3.

- Drop what you're holding and cover your ears
- Stagger back a few steps
- You're deafened and disoriented for a few moments
