# The Frozen Wastes

North of the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#The Whitefang Mountains|Whitefang Mountains]]** lies a vast steppe, a windswept sea of shortgrass, sedge, and lichen. No trees grow here. The landscape is broken only by rocky outcrops, low moraines, and kettle lakes. It rarely snows and almost never rains, but hail is somewhat common.

Caribou and mammoths graze the plateau, preyed on by wolves and saber-toothed cats and terrible things that crawl forth from the lakes in summer. Bands of mammoth herders roam in warmer months. But for the most part, it's an empty and forlorn place.

Farther north, the plateau gets even colder, even windier, the steppe giving way to tundra. Eventually, a mountain-like wall of **[[38 - Rime Lords#Dark ice|dark ice]]** blocks the way, stretching across the horizon. The glacier is the final working of the **[[38 - Rime Lords#Rime Lords|Rime Lords]]**. It seals a rift in the world, and keeps the worst of the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Howling Curse]]**) at bay. Mammoth herders come here to quarry dark ice, slowly chipping the glacier away.

## **Impressions**

#### **Always**

- A landscape so wide and empty you can see the horizon curve
- The constant wind, whispering and howling, tugging and lashing
- Patterns forming in the windswept grass or snow, or the growth of flowers, like runes that fade just before you can read them
- The swell of anger, the urge to inflict violence on your companions, as your temper and patience fray

#### **Spring**

- Yellow and brown grass, splashed with pale violet flowers
- Fields of standing water, just inches deep, reflecting the empty sky
- Squelch and crackle of half-frozen soil underfoot

#### **Summer**

- Days that last forever, the sun never truly setting
- Mix of bright green grass, red lichen, white flowers
- Swarms of insects, spawned from muddy soil
- Birds and **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**1d6** instinct/moves|ice drakes]]** pecking at the soil or flocking to the sky

### **Autumn**

- The world turned red and orange and golden
- Long shadows in the low, low sun
- Sharp cold edge to the wind
- The restless foraging of small animals

### **Winter**

- Endless night, the sun barely rising at noon
- Thin carpet of frost and snow, shrubs and dead grass poking out
- The ceaseless, gnawing wind and cold
- The sense that you've made a terrible mistake in coming here

## **Traversing the Wastes**

The Frozen Wastes are inhospitable in a way that southerners can barely comprehend. On any expedition into the wastes:

- You need a knowledgeable guide, like a **[[19 - The Frozen Wastes#**Dangers**|mammoth herder]]**, who knows the ways of this place
- You need lots of firewood or dried dung, for you'll find no fuel and even in the summer the "nights" can be freezing
- The way will be grueling (from cold and mud and bugs); you risk exhausting yourselves/your supplies
- You need to watch out for predators, like **[[23 - The Great Wood#**Raselbaedd**|wolves]]** or mchebra-gris (**kleztigrs**, basically, see [[42 - The Steplands#1d6 natural beast or spirit|The Steplands]])
- You need to watch out for kettle lakes, easily mistaken for ice or shallow standing water
- You risk succumbing to the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Howling Curse]]**, which taints much of the Wastes

### **Mammoth herders**

Here's a typical band:

**Size** *hamlet* (~15-30 souls) **Population** +0

#### **Prosperity** -1

- Trade with (pick 1 or 2)...
  - ... other mammoth herders
  - ... a farming valley
  - ... a fortress-monastery

#### **Resources**

- Mammoths (wool, ivory, bone, dung)
- Sleds/sledges of bone, hide, timber
- Hunting (wild mammoths, caribou, hare, fish)
- Gathering (timber, ivory, bone, flint, herbs, **[[38 - Rime Lords#Dark ice|dark ice]]**

#### **Defenses** +0

- Mammoth-mounted warriors
- Hounds and hunting drakes
- Spears/javelins tipped with dark ice, flint, bone, or bronze (*crude*)

About half of each band are youngsters, with the rest being able-bodied adults. Elders rarely allow themselves to survive long enough to become a burden to the others. A band typically keeps a herd of 10 to 20 adult **[[19 - The Frozen Wastes#**Dangers**|mammoths]]**, plus a few young. They butcher their own beasts only when desperate, keeping them instead for wool, transport, and labor.

A band spends the warmer months on the Frozen Wastes, but spend the colder months sheltering in the caves, ruins, and forests of the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#The Whitefang Mountains|Whitefang Mountains]]**. They'll trade with **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Questions**|valley farms]]** or **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Monk**|fortress-monasteries]]** in spring, before heading out into the Wastes, or in autumn, before they encamp for the winter.

**Something interesting:** Mammoth herders tend to be impeccably polite, with drawn-out, formal greetings and elaborate, almost artistic ways of doing everything from lacing up a parka to butchering a beast.

**Something useful:** These rituals help identify those afflicted with the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Howling Curse]]**, who lack in patience and good grace. Alas, this means that mammoth herders interpret frustration or bad manners as a sign of being accursed.

# **Dangers**

## **Hazards**

- Brutal cold and ceaseless wind, leading to **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Ice drake**|hypothermia & frostbite]]**
- Hail storms—annoying, pelting (d4 damage), smashing (d6 damage)
- Mud—sucking, cold, exhausting
- Slippery footing
- Still, reflective water hiding a sudden, plunging drop into a kettle
- In summer: swarms of midges/ mosquitos—annoying, distracting, painful, diseased
- Getting lost or misjudging time in the vast featureless terrain
- **[[46 - The Things Below#**Voice of the Eternal Maw**|Whispers and visions]]**, pushing people towards bloodshed, cruelty, and savagery
- Places tainted by the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Howling Curse]]**

### **Monsters**

- **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**1d6** instinct/moves|Ice drakes]]**, especially in warmer months
- **[[42 - The Steplands#1d6 natural beast or spirit|Kleztigrs]]**, called "mchebragris" in the local tongue
- **[[23 - The Great Wood#**Raselbaedd**|Wolves]]**
- A **[[14 - The Flats#**Frythanc**|frythanc]]**, nesting in the Whitefangs and preying on caribou or even mammoths
- **[[10 - Death and the Undying#**Pyped**|Wraiths]]** of those who died of starvation and want, or corrupted by the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Howling Curse]]**
- **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the wild|Spirits of the wild]]**
- **[[38 - Rime Lords#**Tulpa**|Tulpas]]**, free-roaming or bound to something nearby
- **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Shantyara**|Kyakaara]]**, perhaps what remains of a corrupted band of mammoth herders
