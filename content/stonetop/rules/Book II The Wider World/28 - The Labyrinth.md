# The Labyrinth

These tunnels stretch out beneath the **[[27 - Huffel Peaks#Huffel Peaks|Huffel Peaks]]**, occasionally connecting to the surface. Perhaps they stretch further, to the **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#The Whitefang Mountains|Whitefangs]]** or the **[[42 - The Steplands#Spring|Steplands]]** or even to the **[[23 - The Great Wood#The Great Wood|Great Wood]]**.

The **[[51 - Ustrina#Ustrina|Ustrina]]** come out of the Labyrinth when they visit Gordin's Delve to trade. But when asked, they claim it is not their home.

Other things come out of those tunnels, too, to prey on miners in the Delves. Survivors tell tales full of outlandish horror. Chief among those tales are the **[[28 - The Labyrinth#Thraulgwyn|thraulgwyn]]**, the "pale demons," white-haired raiders wielding weapons of bone and volcanic glass.

But the Labyrinth holds more than horrors. It holds riches, too. Ore and gems, seams of coal, and ancient wonders of the Forge Lords. Riches enough to spur the occasional expedition, ill-fated though it may be.

## **Impressions**

- The vast and hostile darkness, a quiet that sucks at the soul
- The humbling scale of the tunnels
- Air heavy with the smell of earth, stone, and sulfur
- Smooth walls, ribbed walls, knobby walls, cracked walls
- Distant dripping, clinking stone, footsteps, whispers, screams

## **Questions**

- Which of you has seen the Labyrinth in your dreams? What do you recall?
- Who entered the Labyrinth years ago, never to be seen again?
- What legendary Forge Lord site is said to be down there?
- Of all the tales that Delvers tell, of things coming out of the Labyrinth, which disturbs you most?
- What do you think the thraulgwyn, the pale demons, really are?

## **Hooks**

- Thraulgwyn raid a mining crew and drag off captives.
- A prospector can lead the PCs to the **[[28 - The Labyrinth#The Portal Vault|Portal Vault]]**, but plans to feed them to her sister's wraith once there.
- Ustrina offer a reward for retrieving a relic from a ruin in the Labyrinth.
- An old map of the Labyrinth shows a path from Gordin's Delve to Moratia Rubra (the Forge Lords' old capitol).
- More and more Delvers hear a voiceless call from the Labyrinth. Some heed it.
- A **[[18 - Forge Lords#**Draventao**|draventao]]** emerges from its lair in the Labyrinth and preys on loggers, miners, and such.

## **Navigating the Labyrinth**

The Labyrinth is vast and sprawling. In theory, one could travel these tunnels from Gordin's Delve to the far end of the Huffel Peaks and the sea that lies beyond. It'd be madness, but one could try.

On any expedition into the Labyrinth:

- You'll need plenty of light sources and more supplies than you'd think
- You need a knowledgeable guide or an accurate map
- Even then, you risk getting lost
  - You must surmount any number of tight squeezes and difficult climbs
- The way is perilous, plagued with danger

## **LOST IN THE DARK**

When you *lose your way in the Labyrinth*, each member of your party consumes 1d4-1 supplies. Then, roll 2d6 once for the party and if…

- … you all had enough supplies, add +1;
- … you are led by someone wise to the Labyrinth's ways, add +1;
- … you are stumbling around blind, roll with disadvantage; and/or
- … one of you has the Mental Map move (from the Ranger playbook), roll with advantage.

**On a 10**+, you find your way to a portable light source, an exit from the Labyrinth, or a familiar path—GM's choice as to which; **on a 7-9**, you encounter **[[28 - The Labyrinth#Thraulgwyn|thraulgwyn]]**, a Forge Lord **[[18 - Forge Lords#1d6 site|ruin]]**, or an obstruction [[28 - The Labyrinth#**Nightmare tableaux**|The Labyrinth]]—GM's choice, and afterwards you might need to make this move again; **on a 6-**, decide as a group whether you find yourselves in the **[[28 - The Labyrinth#The Deeps|Deeps]]**, or stumble out of the Labyrinth days or weeks later, with all your debilities marked and only 1d8 HP remaining. If you choose the latter, each PC must pick 1:

- You have suffered a permanent injury or impairment—what and how?
- A follower of yours didn't make it out—who and why?
- You lost a treasured (or at least vital) item—what and how?
- Actually, you didn't make it out after all —why not?

## **Notable sites**

- Heat, choking air, and a resonant rumble give way to a magma lake, air a-shimmer with heat, toxic gas.
- A vast subterranean lake, perhaps the same one that connects to the **[[22 - Gordin's Delve#**The Delves**|Second Delve]]**.
- The **Hanging Forest**, a long, wide tunnel full of skinny roots dangling from the surface. Full of crickets and things that eat them.

## **The Portal Vault**

Five hours from the Delves (if you know the shortest route) stands a pair of double doors: 30' tall, bronze, covered in runic wards and reliefs of Forge Lord glories. A cunning lock seals them. Only six keys exist; the **[[51 - Ustrina#Ustrina|Ustrina]]** hold five.

Inside is a vast octagonal space, 60' from floor to angled ceiling. Six of its corners feature 20' tall niches, each housing a large **[[18 - Forge Lords#**Metal constructs**|metal construct]]** that attacks any party that doesn't bear one of the keys.

A slab of white stone, 30' tall and 3' thick, stands upright in the center of the vault. Each side is etched with a magic circle. It's a **[[36 - Primordial Powers#**Primordial powers**|portal]]**, connected to those in the **[[39 - The Ruined Tower#The Ruined Tower|Ruined Tower]]**, **[[47 - Three Coven Lake#Three Coven Lake|Three Coven Lake]]**, **[[52 - Vor Svetelik#Vor Svetelik|Vor Svetelik]]**, and so forth. It is fully functional, and can be used via **[[57 - Appendix C - Minor Arcana#A time-worn missive|Opening the Way]]**.

The Ustrina use this place to travel to Gordin's Delve, and to return to their far-flung homes. No one sect controls the Vault, and they treat it as neutral ground. But that doesn't apply to non-Ustrina.

## **Thraulgwyn**

Their hair is white, their skin pallid and painted with lime and mud and blood. Their pupils are wide, like gaping holes. They file their teeth sharp, and craft their weapons from obsidian, stone, and bone. They tan human flesh for leather.

The folks of Gordin's Delve know them as thraulgwyn, "pale demons," terrors that emerge from the Labyrinth and drag off friends and loved ones.

**Something interesting:** The thraulgwyn descended from **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow Builders|Barrow Builders]]**, from the followers of certain warlords and sorcerer-kings who fled into the Labyrinth when their subjects rose against them. They were already depraved disciples of the Things Below, and after generations of inbreeding, cave-dwelling, and cannibalism, they're barely human.

**Something useful:** Except, that's not really true. They have language (a dialect of the Hillfolk tongue) and outsiders have made deals with them. They hate other tribes more than they hate surface-dwellers. And while they do eat the dead, most of their food is foraged, even cultivated.

**Tip:** Cock your head. Dart your eyes. Hold a hand up to block the light. Try not to touch your teeth with your tongue.

# **The Deeps**

The Labyrinth is far from static. Earthquakes, volcanism, and burrowing **[[28 - The Labyrinth#**Magma worm**|magma worms]]** all wreak havoc upon it. But you could map it. You could learn its ways and find your way back to a place you had been.

But as one goes deeper, things change. The world becomes… softer. Less fixed. And beyond an uncertain point, one enters the Deeps.

The Deeps shift when you aren't looking. They reflect your fears and your desires. They seek to trap you, to twist you, to consume you. For it is here, of all the places in the world, that one is closest to the Things Below.

Why would anyone come here? Perhaps…

- … they are called by the Things Below;
- … they are lost and didn't mean to come;
- … to rescue someone;
- … to find an artifact of legend;
- … to hide something away forever; and/or
- … to do the impossible: to face and truly slay one of the Things Below.

## **Impressions**

- Endless tunnels, going and going
- Thoughts drifting as you trudge along, jerking awake
- The darkness, deeper and absolute
- When did you last eat, drink, or rest?
- Wait, who are these people again?
- Dreamlike acceptance of whatever weirdness you encounter

- Who'd be better off if you never made it home?

#### **Traversing the Deeps**

Time flows strangely down here. Visitors don't really need food or drink. Sleep is fleeting. Light sources might last indefinitely, only to snuff out at the worst moment. Distances are fluid, dreamlike. Those who wander without a purpose are sure to be trapped, or worse.

#### **The Utterwell**

At the very bottom of the Deeps stands a throne of **[[36 - Primordial Powers#**Primordial powers**|black iron]]**, the seat of **[[28 - The Labyrinth#**Soithech**|Tcaventes, Shackle and Key]]**. Behind the throne looms a black iron door. An **[[18 - Forge Lords#**1d6** architectural elements|orichalcum]]** bolt holds the door fast. The bolt is bound with the **[[58 - Appendix D - Major Arcana#Ineffable Words|Ineffable Word]]** for "seal."

The black iron door opens onto the shore of the Utterwell: a sunless sea, still and black as night. No light penetrates its depths, not even the light of Helior. Anyone or anything who enters the sea is gone, consumed by the Things Below.

When you *stand before the Utterwell and call one of the Things Below by name*, it will emerge in all its terrible glory. Woe be upon you. And if the door is still open, woe be upon the world.

## **Magma worm**

 *Solitary*, *primordial*, *large*, *terrifying* **HP** 19; **Armor** 4 (rocky plates, lacks vitals) **Damage** bite d10+3 (*hand*, *close*, *grabby*, *messy*, 1 piercing) or searing touch d10 (*hand*, *area*)

**Special qualities** blind; tremorsense; fireproof; superheated body; can move only forward, can't back up

**Instinct** to devour minerals and metal

- ä Eat through solid rock
- ä Burst forth from a tunnel wall
- ä Exude choking, sulfurous gas
- ä Melt metal that pierces it

Creatures of the first age, things of fire and stone. They're segmented, 3' thick and 20' long, covered in overlapping plates that glow red at the seams. The air heat-shimmers around them. They have no eyes, just a great mouth-opening and concentric rows of grinding teeth that melt and burrow through rock. Winding tunnels stretch behind them, lined with slag hardened into ropy stone.

The worms described here are mere centuries old, the ones most likely to be encountered. Older and larger ones survive in the Deeps, up to 10' wide and over a 100' long (*huge*, +6 HP, +2 damage and roll 1d12 instead of 1d10). In eons past, even larger worms carved out much of the Labyrinth.

## **Thraulgwyn raider**

 *Group*, *corrupted*, *stealthy*, *organized*, *cautious*

**HP** 6; **Armor** 0 or 1 (human hide) **Damage** obsidian-tipped bone spear d8 (*close*, *thrown*, *crude*), femur d8 (*close*, *crude*), hair-rope net (*thrown*, *crude*, *grabby*), bite d6 (*hand*)

**Special qualities** gifted and marked according to their tribe [[28 - The Labyrinth#1d6 discovery|1d6]]

**Instinct** to glorify their tribe

- ä Use their gift
- ä Move quietly through the dark
- ä Lure prey into an ambush
- ä Pounce with sudden ferocity
- ä Flee if things go south

They roam the tunnels in gangs of 3-5, playing cat and mouse with rival bands. Should they notice outsiders, they might try to ambush them, capture them, and eat them. But they might also greet them, offer to trade, try to use them against their foes.

Some raiding parties are led by a champion (*leader*, +2 HP, deals damage with advantage), who bears one of their tribal band's sacred artifacts.

## **Soithech**

*Solitary*, *corrupted*, *devious*, *magical*, *cautious*

**HP** 18; **Armor** 5 (resilience, human hide), 1 vs. bronze

**Damage** obsidian knife d10 (*hand*, *crude*) **Special qualities** powerfully gifted and marked, as per their tribe [[28 - The Labyrinth#1d6 discovery|1d6]]

**Instinct** to maintain their status

- ä Foresee an opportunity or dire event
- ä Play parties against each other
- ä Use their gift, or one of their tribe's sacred artifacts
- ä Embody an aspect of their master

Most thraulgwyn settlements are ruled by a soithech, a "vessel," who claims descent from the tribe's founder. They preach old ways and old hatreds, keeping their people in line with prophecies of glory and doom—some of which are even true.

A soitech's power is not just political. They manifest their tribe's gift and mark with great intensity, and they can let the power of their patron Thing Below flow through them. When they do, they change to embody one of its aspects [[46 - The Things Below#**Emanation**|The Things Below]] and gain up to 3 additional gifts [[46 - The Things Below#**1d12** origin|The Things Below]]. Adjust their stat block accordingly.

## **Tcaventes, Shackle and Key**

 *Solitary*, *large*, *primordial*, *construct*, *magical*, *terrifying*, *corrupted*

**HP** 25; **Armor** 6 (hard, warded, lacks vitals), 4 vs. black iron

**Damage** word of force d12+3 (*near*, *area*, *forceful*) or four fists d12+3 with advantage (*close*, *reach*, *forceful*)

**Special qualities** ageless; immune to the elements; perceives thought and matter; omnilingual

**Instinct** to hide evidence of their failure, even from themselves

- ä Sense and neutralize threats with cold precision
- ä Shift the environment to create or close an opening
- ä Seal or unseal: a door, a binding, a mouth, a secret, etc.
- ä Blaze suddenly with the blinding light of creation

They are an **[[36 - Primordial Powers#**Other primordial entities**|archon]]**, forged by the First Creators to stand vigil over the **[[28 - The Labyrinth#**Tcaventes, Shackle and Key**|Utterwell]]** and to act as a bulwark against the Things Below. They are Tcaventes, Shackle and Key. A flawless guardian.

Or at least, they were. Over eons, even they succumbed to the whispers of the Things Below. The corruption leads to lapses of discipline that let their charges' influence seep out through the Deeps. It amplifies their guilt, which leads to denial strong enough to drive Tcaventes mad.

Should the PCs encounter Tcaventes on their throne, they will find the archon focused, vigilant, and stern, with only a hint of their unravelling mind.
