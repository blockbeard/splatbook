# Vor Svetelik

The **[[24 - Green Lords#Green Lords|Green Lords]]** called this place "the Great White City." It was a place of beauty, learning, and study. But when the **[[12 - Fae#Fae|Fae]]** rebelled, the scholars of Vor Svetelik were tasked with breeding beasts of war. When the Green Lords turned in desperation to the **[[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|Things Below]]**, the city's waters fouled and its white trees grew sickly. When other **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]** shattered what is now **[[13 - Ferrier's Fen#Ferrier's Fen|Ferrier's Fen]]**, this city became the Green Lords' last refuge and the site of the war's last battle.

The Green Lords made the invaders pay. They laid traps. They sent their loyal servants to die in waves. They unleashed new horrors, each worse than the last, retreating all the while. And when the Fae and their **[[43 - Stone Lords#Stone Lords|Stone Lord]]** allies reached the center of the city, they found it… empty. Naught remained of the Green Lords, except tunnels leading deep into the earth, collapsed behind them.

The Stone Lords retreated to their hills. The Fae fell to bickering and their armies disbanded. Those few mortal servants of the Green Lords who survived were left to fend for themselves, eventually becoming the **[[17 - Forest Folk#Forest Folk|Forest Folk]]**.

And Vor Svetelik, the Great White City, was left a blighted, infested ruin.

## **Impressions**

- A scraggly canopy in the valley below, hints of streets and buildings, a broken white tower
- Pale and spindly trees, bearing withered leaves and oozing black sap
- A steady, damp warmth, like foul breath, even in the dead of winter
- That feeling you get when you move a rock and see the grubs and worms squirming beneath, but constantly, everywhere you look

## **Hooks**

- An artifact that the PCs desperately want lies in the city's ruins
- A southern **[[29 - Lygos and the South#Antiquarian|antiquarian]]** knows where to find the ruins, and is leading a substantial expedition there, using Stonetop as a basecamp
- **[[12 - Fae#The Four Chieftains|A Fae chieftain]]** is besieging the ruins, attempting to finally exterminate the Fomoraij
- An earthquake cracks open the ruins; horrors vomit forth into the surrounding woods

## **Getting there**

The ruins of Vor Svetelik lie in the **[[23 - The Great Wood#The Great Wood|Great Wood]]**, wherever you (the GM) need them to be. They should be at least a few days' travel from Stonetop.

To find the ruins, PCs will need a guide or detailed directions.

- A Fae, a **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the Wild|spirit of the wild]]**, or the **[[17 - Forest Folk#Forest Folk|Forest Folk]]** could guide them, but wouldn't want to. All sane beings shun the ruins.
- A Green Lord ruin might contain a map or directions, but the PCs would risk getting lost. Such instructions would be centuries out of date!
- Magic like the **[[57 - Appendix C - Minor Arcana#^a-patch-of-rainbow-moss|Sylvan Web]]** could help the PCs find the ruins themselves, though they'd still risk getting lost on the way.
- The **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|West Road]]** once led from where Stonetop stands to Vor Svetelik. It's broken and long-buried, but could show the way to one who knew what to look for.

## **Areas**

- **Outer slopes of the hills surrounding the city**, jagged, overgrown, littered with Forest Folk glyphs and invaders' graves.
- **Inner slopes of those same hills**, steep and unstable, trees thinning out as one descends. Signs of old battle abound. **[[43 - Stone Lords#Some examples:|Stone Lord constructs]]** still stand watch.
- **The city's outskirts**, hiding ruins of manor-houses and plantations.
- **The city's center**, tightly packed with ruins, corpses, and danger.
- **The undercity**, old sewers, cisterns, tunnels, basements, and vaults; linked via erosion, collapse, and beast-burrows.

## **Sites**

The following can all be found in the city's center, though still some distance apart.

- **The Conservatory**, once a latticed dome of living wood and glass, now burnt, broken, shattered. Bound fertility spirits mournfully tend to **[[24 - Green Lords#Hazardous growth|hazardous growths]]**.
- **The Menagerie**, mostly intact. Cubbies line the walls, each with tissue (fur, tooth, etc.) from a different beast. A plasmic horror dwells in a central pit.
- **The Great Library**, broken white tower, poking through the canopy. Lower levels largely intact—grand, overgrown. Heavily warded, a few chambers still perfectly preserved. Littered with the remains of invaders.
- **The Necropolis**, cluster of ziggurats, domes, and monuments near the city's center. Ransacked and vandalized, with few tombs intact. Deeply haunted.
- **The Hallow Tree**, amphitheater carved from a single, massive, fossilized tree stump. Green Lords once met here to share and debate. All that remains is a basalt slab, piles and piles of bones, and hints of tunnels long-ago collapsed.

### **1d12** surface terrain

- 1 Stretch of true death, where nothing has grown or decayed for hundreds of years

- 4-5 Stretch of sickly white trees

- 2 Running water
- 3-4 Old tunnels/shafts, some stretching for miles
- 5-6 green lord ruin (see next page) or part of one, buried/sunken/toppled\*

- 9-10 Stairs/shaft/ladder/ramp, going up and/or further down\*
- 11 Crevasse, maybe open to the sky, maybe not\*
- 12 Sinkhole, leading down down down

### **1d12** green lord ruin

- 1 Arched, petrified trees, forming a half-cylinder. Largely collapsed. The remains shelter a stone slab (3 by 6 paces) and empty canopic jars.
- 2 Buried vault, cracked, mostly full of dirt. Inner walls lined with bas relief vines in runic shapes.
- 3 Crumbling walls of a ziggurat. "Inside" of walls bear shifting mosaics of colorful moss, showing history of the Fae.
- 4 Earth-covered mound. Entrance sealed by living roots (part with command word). Purple crystals line interior, gently glowing.
- 5 Foundation of white/green/gray stones, hints of faded scrollwork, hinting at long-gone structures.
- 6 Half-buried dome, ~15 paces across. Exposed timbers of uncut tree trunks. Broken gate of woven antler.

### **1d12** green lord ruin

- 7 Half-dozen structures of rust-red trees, grown in beehive shapes. Untouched by time. Inside: cauldrons, misshapen bones, carapaces.
- 8 House-sized pile of dirt, hints of worked stone beneath. Inside: a modest dwelling lit by glowing ambers.
- 9 Intact cellar, too small for Makers. Door is stuck, but works. Lit by glowing lichen. Holds ancient foodstuffs; crumbles at touch or breeze.
- 10 Massive tree trunk, ~10 paces wide, broken off at top. Remaining wood glints of silver. Entrance to hollow is embossed with ivory runes. Black sap drips from interior walls.
- 11 Ramparts of iron-infused trees, sporting long thorns, blocking what once were streets. Rusted, crumbling, uprooted in places.
- 12 Underground "cage" made of tree roots infused with iron. Still intact, no apparent entrances.

The above are just examples. See page 214 if you need to create more or different ruins.

### **The cracked portal**

Somewhere deep in the ruins is a huge slab of white rock, etched with a magic circle. A large crack mars the stone and the circle, yet they still thrum with power.

The circle is a **[[36 - Primordial Powers#Portals|portal]]**, connected to those beneath the **[[39 - The Ruined Tower#The Ruined Tower|Ruined Tower]]**, the **[[28 - The Labyrinth#The Labyrinth|Labyrinth]]**, near **[[47 - Three Coven Lake#Three Coven Lake|Three Coven Lake]]**, and so forth. The portal can be opened (see **[[57 - Appendix C - Minor Arcana#^a-time-worn-missive|Opening the Way]]**), but it is dangerous and unstable.

When you *open the cracked portal* (from either direction), roll +INT: **on a 10+**, the portal opens but pick 1 from the list below; **on a 7-9**, the portal opens but pick 2:

- Any who traverse it risk serious harm —1d10 damage (*messy*, ignores armor)
- You draw the attention of some dangerous primordial entity, like an **[[36 - Primordial Powers#Archons|archon]]** or a **[[36 - Primordial Powers#Voidblight|voidblight]]**
- The cracks grow bigger; subsequent attempts to use this portal will have a cumulative -1 penalty

**On a 6-**, the cracked portal shatters violently (1d10 damage, *reach*, *area*, *messy*, *forceful*, ignores armor) and leaves a **[[36 - Primordial Powers#Rift|rift]]** in its place. The other portal cracks (or shatters, if it was already cracked).

## **Dangers**

### **Hazards**

- Ground collapsing underfoot, dropping (deeper) into the undercity
- A dangerous climb—up or down—and a reason to attempt it
- Spells of war, trapped in stasis, crackling in the air and waiting to spring
- Weird distortions of time and space
- Fae **[[12 - Fae#Illusions and glamours|glamours]]**, concealing a place or waiting to ensnare
- **[[24 - Green Lords#Hazardous growth|Hazardous growths]]**, run rampant or deployed as defenses

### **Podburst vines**

As enemies prepared to invade, the Green Lords layered their city with living boobytraps. Many remain. The most common are fast-growing vines that bear large, ominous pods.

- Many tendrils cover a structure or path, obvious or hidden beneath soil/brush
- The tendrils tense and writhe when something disturbs them
- A seedpod contracts, then **explodes** (*reach*, *area*, see below)
- A chain reaction starts, one pod exploding after another

### **1d6** explodes with...

What do the exploding pods do? Pick 1 or roll.

| 1d6 | Explodes with...                                                                    |
|-----|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1   | … iron-infused seeds, like shrapnel (d10 damage, *messy*, 1 piercing)               |
| 2   | … concussive force (d4+2 damage, *forceful*, *loud*)                                |
| 3   | … seeds that rapidly burst into thin, thorny, ensnaring vines (d6 damage, *grabby*) |
| 4   | … a cloud of spores that blind/sicken/choke/etc. (d4 damage, *debilitating*)        |
| 5   | … pollen that glows and/or attracts horrors (like **blade wasps**, next page)       |
| 6   | … iron-infused spore-dust, clinging to any it touches                               |

### **Deathgrip vines**

In some parts of the city, you'll find hunks of stone cracked apart by vines. The vines bear clusters of burrs all up and down their stalks, which easily fall off and cling to clothing, gear, hair, even skin. The burrs are annoying, maybe a little painful, but not (immediately) harmful.

Throughout the ruins, though, are old sigils of power. When these burrs are brought within a few paces of the sigils, they rapidly sprout and start to grow. And burrow. And grip. And tear.

When you *try to free yourself of sprouting deathgrip vines*, roll +STR or +DEX (your call): **on a 10+**, you can get free if you pick 1 from the list below; **on a 7-9**, you can get free if you pick 2:

- Tear vines from your flesh (1d8 damage, *messy*, ignores armor)
- Lose/discard 1d4 × ◇ worth of items
- Someone else (who isn't in a similar plight) needs to help you

**On a 6-**, take 1d6 damage (*messy*, *grabby*, ignores armor) and the vines continue to grow, burrow, and constrict. Each time they deal damage, increase the damage die by one step.

### **Blade wasps**

Like wasps, but bigger and with iron-infused stingers. Hey, at least they're not venomous.

When you *are stung by a single blade wasp*, it hurts, draws blood, and takes some effort not to cry out.

When you *get swarmed by blade wasps*, take 1d6 damage (*messy*, ignores armor) and you're wracked with pain. Doing anything other than curling up and writhing means that you're Defying Danger (likely with CON). Each subsequent time you take damage from the swarm, increase the die one step (max d12).

When you *flee a blade wasp swarm*, roll +CON: **on a 10+**, you escape the swarm but pick 1 from the list below; **on a 7-9**, you escape the swarm but pick 2:

- Suffer damage from the swarm
- Mark a debility from blood loss
- An important piece of gear is lost or ruined
- You run straight into another danger
