## **The Compact**

For generations, the people of Stonetop have kept their promise to the **[[17 - Forest Folk#Forest Folk|Forest Folk]]**: they never cut down a living tree in the Great Wood. The villagers gather deadfall, but timber comes from elsewhere.

- According to the elders, when did someone last break the compact? What happened to them?
- Who's been pushing to start harvesting trees, since the Forest Folk seem to have vanished?

What will happen if someone fells a living tree? That's for you to decide. Maybe…

- … they suffer the **[[37 - Red Groves#**Why are they so special?**|Forest's Wrath]]**;
- … they risk releasing an evil spirit, held captive by the tree's roots;
- … the trees house the spirits of dead Forest Folk, and they've just created a **[[10 - Death and the Undying#**Pyped**|specter]]**; or
- … nothing—unless the Forest Folk return.

Requires all of the following:

- Proof that trees can be felled without dire consequence
- A crew of brave lumberjacks
- A skilled carpenter, working full time
- Pulling Together once, over a course of a few weeks, to generate the first load of timber.

## **Cave bear**

 *Solitary*, *large*, *cautious*

**HP** 16; **Armor** 1 (fur, hide)

**Damage** claws, bite, hug d10+4 (*hand*, *close*, *forceful*, *messy*, 1 piercing)

**Instinct** to fill its belly, to protect its young

- ä Rend, maul, crush
- ä Move with surprising speed and grace
- ä Sniff out food, or trouble

Great big silver-brown things, eating anything they can dig up or catch. They mostly hibernate through winter, and get especially bold and ornery in autumn as they try to fatten up. They've been known to wake up and forage even in winter, though.

They lair in big caves, usually two mates and maybe their cubs. Out and about, the bulls are usually found on their own; the sows on their own, or with their cubs. They're not usually aggressive towards people—unless surprised, extra hungry, or protecting the cubs. Then? Watch out.

## **Cwisarff**

 *Solitary*, *small*, *stealthy*, *cautious*

**HP** 10; **Armor** 0

**Damage** venomous bite d10 (*close*) **Instinct** to defend its current spot

- ä Wait patiently to spring on prey
- ä Hiss menacingly at a threat
- ä Bite quickly and ready to strike again

The vaunted "tree adder," most lethal serpent in the Wood, up to 6 feet long and dripping with venom. In warm months, they hunt and bask in the canopy or sunny clearings. In colder months, they hide in hollows and under logs, going into torpor in winter. They don't hunt people, but they don't fear them either.

When you are bitten by a cwisarff, mark a debility in addition to taking damage. Until the poison is treated, you can't regain HP and are in danger of further damage and debility.

## **Cynddaraig**

 *Solitary*, *large*, *terrifying*, *fearless* **HP** 20; **Armor** 2 (scales)

**Damage** three-inch long teeth d10+3 (*close*, *reach*, *grabby*, *messy*, 3 piercing, advantage)

- **Instinct** to dominate its territory ä Roar and unfurl its quills, making even brave warriors quail
- ä Bite a limb clean off
- ä To lose itself in primal fury

The mighty "rage drake," almost 10 feet tall and 20 feet from nose to tip of tail. Its jaws are full of knives. Blood-red patterns surround its eyes. A frill of feathered quills sprouts from its neck and runs down its back. Maybe a half-dozen of them stalk the whole Great Wood, and one hasn't been spotted near Stonetop for generations (Tor be praised).

**Something interesting:** They prey mostly on large game (wisents, tal gerdwyr, etc.). They'll attack people, but rarely hunt them.

**Something useful:** They attack people to establish dominance, seeing us as rivals. An appropriate offering or show of obeisance might calm one enough for you to get the hell out of there.

## **Gylglyd vines**

 **Damage** strangling vines d6 (*hand*, *grabby*)

## **Instinct** to lure prey

Thick, velvety green vines that drape entire groves or clearings, speckled with flowers of iridescent blue and pink and yellow. The mind reels to see them, wanting to move closer. #### **Raselbaedd**

 *Solitary*, *large*

**HP** 20; **Armor** 4 (hide, unnatural resilience) **Damage** gore d10+5 (*hand*, *close*, *forceful*, *messy*, 3 piercing)

**Special qualities** iron hide, always hungry, easily provoked, afraid of fire

**Instinct** to hold a grudge

- ä Rip things apart: armor, weapons, flesh
- ä Eat almost anything
- ä Refuse to back off or let go, even when dying
- ä Recover from even mortal wounds, over time

When a boar lives long enough, and survives enough close calls, and dines year after year on the roots and nuts of ferro trees, it eventually becomes a raselbaedd. The size of a horse, with tusks like short spears and a hide that's crisscrossed with scars and that bristles with broken blades.

When you *deal 2 damage or less to a raselbaedd*, *before armor,* your weapon chips or shatters on its hide.

A raselbaedd's hide (*immobile*, Value 2) can be harvested by a competent hunter with a fine steel blade (2 piercing, Value 2). It can then be fashioned into 1d4 raselbaedd cuirasses (2 armor, Value 3). Doing so requires:

- An exceptional tanner
- An upgraded workshop with fine steel tools (Value 3)
- A season of labor per cuirass
