# Artifact Creation

Use this procedure when you need an artifact, but none of the existing options fit. Or, use it when you have an idea that you want to flesh out. As you work through each step, take notes on your decisions or results.

Pick or roll for the artifact's origin.

Then, pick or roll for one or more themes from the indicated entry. Allow the theme(s) to inform everything else about this artifact.

Pick one or roll, then proceed to the indicated sub-step and follow the directions there.

### **2a. Mundane**

It's no magical wonder, but it's still interesting, useful, and/or valuable. Pick or roll for why they might care.

### **1d12** why they might care

- 1 It'd make a good trophy/gift/thing to show the kids (Value 0)
- 2 It's useful—like a tool, a weapon, supplies, etc. (Value 0 or 1)
- 3 It's useful but also unusual, specialized, uncommon (Value 1)
- 4 It's fine, beautiful, excellent (Value 1)
- 5 It's of a distinct origin/style (Value 1 or 2 to a collector)
- 6 It's inherently valuable—silver, trade goods, etc. (Value 2)
- 7 It's a true luxury and/or a symbol of status (Value 2)
- 8 It's precious, but only to the right buyer (Value 2 or 3)
- 9 It's exquisitely made, a thing of beauty (Value 3)
- 10 It's inherently valuable—gold, gems, exotic goods, etc. (Value 3)
- 11 It's a clue, or it hints at the bigger picture, and roll 1d10 again
- 12 It presents an opportunity: a key, a resource they might mine, a map, etc.

Now, go pick or roll for its **[[13 - Discoveries#3. Form|form]]** step 3).

### **2d. Extraordinary property**

Pick or roll one. Remember to keep the theme in mind!

### **1d12** property

- 1 Moves and acts of its own accord
- 2 Absorbs \_\_\_, stores its power (fire, lightning, hatred, stillness, etc.)
- 3 Seamlessly blends materials in an impossible way
- 4 Emits \_\_\_ (flame, light, sound, silence, a feeling, etc.)
- 5 Is much, much \_\_\_ than it should be (heavier, harder, faster, quieter, etc.)
- 6 Cannot be \_\_\_ (broken, burned, melted, detected, etc.)
- 7 Fills those who look upon it with \_\_\_ (greed, rage, peace, joy, etc.)
- 8 Cuts right through/can harm \_\_\_ (stone, ghosts, etc.)
- 9 Can only/cannot be perceived by \_\_\_ (Fae, spirits, the dead, murderers, etc.)
- 10 Glows/thrums/vibrates in the presence of \_\_\_ (gold, magic, lies, poison, etc.)
- 11 Protects against/repels \_\_\_ (emotional magic, fire/heat, ghosts, etc.)

### **2e. Spirit or sentience**

The artifact serves as a **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Tethers|tether]]** for a spirit, or otherwise acts as its home. Pick or roll for the nature of this binding.

### **1d12** type of binding

- 1-2 Imprisoned and locked away (though imperfectly)
- 3-5 Fettered, enslaved, forced to serve
- 6-7 Serves willingly, bound by its own nature/desires
- 8-9 Trapped by accident, desperation, twist of fate
- 10-12 Dwells here naturally, spontaneously

Then, pick a spirit or sentience appropriate to the binding, origin, and theme. If you're unsure, roll 1d12.

### **1d12** spirit/sentience

- 1 A *corrupted* spirit [[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|The Things Below]], and roll again
- 2 A **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Ghosts|ghost]]**
- 3 A **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Shade|shade]]** or similar imprint of someone's personality
- 4 Something from beyond the Last Door, like a **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Dool spirit|dool spirit]]** or **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Gwyllgi|gwyllgi]]**
- 5 A **[[12 - Fae#Fae|Fae]]**, oddly enough
- 6 A **[[20 - Gods and Religion#Little gods|little god]]**
- 7 A **[[38 - Rime Lords#Tulpa|tulpa]]**, or the intellect of a **[[18 - Forge Lords#Metal constructs|construct]]**
- 8 A **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the Wild|spirit of the wild]]**
- 9 An **emanation** of a Thing Below [[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|The Things Below]]
- 10 A fledgling spirit, and roll 1d8+1 again
- 11 An **[[36 - Primordial Powers#Archons|archon]]** or other **[[36 - Primordial Powers#Primordial powers|primordial entity]]**
- 12 A multitude of... (roll again)

Now, go pick or roll for it's **[[13 - Discoveries#3. Form|form]]** step 3).

### **2f. Source of lore**

Pick or roll for the knowledge imparted and how it's recorded.

### **1d12** knowledge imparted

- 1 The truth/secrets behind a group or figure of legend
- 2 The weakness/history/origins/lair of a major established threat
- 3-4 The hazards/secrets/history/location of a mysterious or legendary place
- 5-6 The workings/secrets/history/location of a magical item or arcanum
- 7-9 An artifice or technique lost to antiquity
- 10-12 The workings of a ritual/spell—pick or roll for its function and drawback [[56 - Appendix B - Artifact Creation#^magical-effect-2g|Appendix B - Artifact Creation]], then return here

### **1d12** how it's recorded

- 1 Known to an entity bound to the artifact; go back to **spirit or sentience** (previous page, step 2e), and do what it says
- 2-5 Inscribed on/in the artifact: ancient runes or writing in a long-dead language
- 6-7 Adorning the artifact: carvings/paintings/decorations/diagrams
- 8-9 Scrawled on/in the artifact: a cypher, mad ramblings, strange annotations, etc.
- 10 As a vision/dream, imparted on whoever touches/enters/approaches
- 11-12 Inscribed on/in the artifact: clear to those who can read the language

### **2g. Magical effect**

| 2    | Defend/repel/secure       |

| 4    | Contain/bind/capture      |

| 8    | Sense/identify/reveal     |

### **1d12** drawback

- 1 Scarring: leaves a lasting thematic mark on you/the target
- 2 Dangerous: can have destructive, unwanted side effects
- 3 Demanding: takes great effort to cast/trigger/maintain
- 4 Discordant: draws unwanted attention
- 5 Fickle: fails at inopportune times
- 6 Weak: easily countered by will/fortitude/preparation
- 7 Indiscriminate: imprecise in its targets/duration/effect
- 8 Withering: weakens you/the artifact/the target
- 9 Restricted: works only in specific, thematic conditions
- 10 Costly: requires sacrifice, consumes resources
- 11 Slow: takes time to cast/manifest
- 12 Roll twice

For artifacts that produce a magical effect (as opposed to spells or rituals), how often or frequently can it be used? Pick or roll.

### **1d12** usage

- 1-3 1d6 uses, then it's consumed/ruined/powerless
- 4-6 1d6 uses, then it must be recharged/replenished
- 7-8 A few charges, which might be lost with each use; recharges with time or effort
- 9 At will, but each time is riskier/less powerful/more costly; resets with effort or slowly over time
- 10 At will, but there's a cost/risk with each use
- 11 At will, though limited by practicalities
- 12 Continuous; it's always going

Now, go to step **3**, below, to pick or roll for its **form**.

### **3. Form** 
If it's not already established or clear to you, pick or roll for the artifact's size.

### **1d12** size

- 1-2 A small item
- 3-4 A ◇ item or object
- 5-6 A ◇◇ item or object
- 7-8 An *immobile* object or place
- 9-10 Roll 1d8 again, but it's hard to extract, access, and/or take advantage of
- 11-12 Roll 1d10 again, but it's also *crude*, *fragile*, *cumbersome*, and/or *dangerous*

Now, what is it, exactly? Pick or roll. Interpret the results liberally, keeping in mind the origin, theme, and other details about the item. Let yourself be inspired!

Note: an artifact might very well be made from or with an appropriate **[[56 - Appendix B - Artifact Creation#2b. Strange material|strange material]]**, step 2b), even if you didn't get that result. Maybe your Forge Lord sword that absorbs heat and flame is made of aetherium, or has a red crystal for its pommel.

### **1d12** what is it?

- 1-8 Something more common (next page)
- 9-12 Something less common (next page)

- 2 Construction: nail, brick, tile, trowel, timber, masonry, wall, etc.

- 4 Domestic: utensil, spindle, vessel, broom, foodstuffs, crib, loom, etc.

### **4. Synthesis** 
Consider the details you've established. Imagine the artifact that they suggest. Fill in any missing details and start making final decisions. What is this artifact, exactly? What was it originally for? How did it come to be, or be *here*?

You don't need a full history of the item. But try to picture something that makes sense, something that at least hints at a story.

As you make these decisions, it's okay to change or discard some of the details you established earlier, or to interpret them loosely or metaphorically. They're meant to spur your creativity, not to limit it.

### **5. Write-up** 
Once you've got a clear picture in your mind, write up the artifact as one of the following:

- A **brief description** and some tags, like ~20 words or fewer
- A **detailed description**, with a title, some tags, and a few sentences about what it is, what it looks like, what it does (if anything), what it involves
- A brief or detailed description (as above), plus one or more **custom player moves**, each with a trigger, results, and maybe a roll
- A **steading improvement**, something that, with time and effort, can provide lasting benefit to the village.
- A **minor arcanum**, a little card you give the player with a description and requirements on the front, and the benefits of meeting them on the back.
- A **major arcanum**: a half-page playbook insert, with unlockable moves/options and consequences for using them.

Keep in mind who you're writing this up for. Anything you give to the players (tags, physical descriptions, custom moves, arcana) should be clear and concise. But an artifact's history, backstory, details on where it's found, the nuances of how it works… that's all for you, to inform how you introduce it and use it in play. Write out as much as you, personally, find helpful.

If the artifact's Value hasn't been established, consider whether it should have one and what it might be. See *[[03 - Playing the Game#Value|Book I]]* for rough guidelines.

If you expect the artifact will be a source of trouble, also write it up as a threat (a *MacGuffin*, probably, or maybe a *magical entity* or even an *affliction*).

Refer to the artifacts throughout this book for examples. See *[[13 - Discoveries#Buying artifacts|Book I]]* for more guidance and some worked examples.
