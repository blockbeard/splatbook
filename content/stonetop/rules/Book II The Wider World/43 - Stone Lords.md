# Stone Lords

In the time of the **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]**, the Stone Lords ruled the **[[42 - The Steplands#The Steplands|Steplands]]**, the southern stretch of the **[[27 - Huffel Peaks#Huffel Peaks|Huffel Peaks]]**, and the **[[40 - South Manmarch#South Manmarch|South Manmarch]]**. They carved cities out of the hills. They raised towering monuments. They sang stone, crystal, and glass into impossible shapes.

The Stone Lords' empire was grand, but it was not all glory. It was built on the backs of countless human servants. And while the Stone Lords were not especially cruel, neither were they kind. They imposed order on their world, by whatever means necessary.

For, better than the other Makers, the Stone Lords knew the**[[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|Things Below]]**. Knew the insidious songs that echoed in the deep. Knew the chaos promised. Knew that they must never, ever be freed.

And so, when the **[[24 - Green Lords#Names|Green Lords]]** called on the Things Below to put down their own servants' rebellion, the Stone Lords turned on their brethren. They shattered the Green Lords' lands and hunted down the survivors.

Yet: the seals had cracked. The Things Below sang all the louder. The Stone Lords themselves stood resolute, but their servants did not. Corruption seeped through their society like water finding cracks in a poorly laid foundation.

The end came swiftly. Sorcerers turned the city of Dulenn into a massive sinkhole, creating what is now **[[06 - Blackwater Lake#Blackwater Lake|Blackwater Lake]]**. Seals were broken across the land and horrors slithered up through the tunnels. Mobs turned on their former masters. One by one, the domains and cities of the Stone Lords fell into ruin.

**Names**

Breton- and Cornish-inspired, but with extra vowels for a drawn-out sound: Aenedellion, Caedour, Daenizel, Eelowain, Gaeille, Iaves, Kaeteal, Loiawen, Mearauwd, Neassa, Oaneaz, Paerrain, Rozainne, Saennaen, Taenguey, Wainoac, Yainniac

Combine with prefixes like "Aalz" ("the great"), "Skol" ("master"), or "Loaji" ("apprentice"), etc.

The**[[55 - Appendix A - Ages of the World#Time of Cataclysm|Time of Cataclysm]]** had arrived.

#### **A chalky potion**

*magical*, *fragile*, *slow*

1d4 glass vials containing thick, gritty, whitish fluid. The stopper bears a mark of transformation, flesh, and stone.

When you *drink the whole potion while touching stone*, you transform (over a matter of minutes) into a mass of stone, similar to that which you touch. You retain awareness of your surroundings, blind but with excellent hearing.

When you *attempt to end your lithic vigil*, roll +CON: **on a 10+**, you return to your original form, rested and invigorated—regain all your HP and clear all your debilities; **on a 7-9**, the transformation back is slow and arduous, mark a debility; **on a 6-**, you are stuck until someone pours another dose of the potion over you or communes with your spirit and helps you try again.

#### **Power-crystal**

, *magical*, *dangerous*

 A chunk of crystal the size of a clenched fist. If charged, it thrums or crackles with power. If depleted, it fills you with a sense of emptiness and longing.

When you *brandish the depleted powercrystal at a source of elemental energy*, roll +CON: **on a 10+**, the crystal absorbs the energy fully and as safely as possible (it is now charged); **on a 7-9**, the crystal absorbs the energy (it's now charged) but pick 1:

- You are harmed by the coursing energy, taking damage as the GM sees fit
- The power stored in the crystal is extremely unstable, likely to discharge without warning
- The crystal cracks; henceforth, treat a 10+ on this move as a 7-9, and you can't pick this option again.

In the hands of a skilled arcanist, a charged power-crystal can fuel or empower a magical effect (though this is dangerous). In the hands of the incautious, the reckless, or the desperate, it can split or crack, leading to a violent explosion.

### **A scarred slab of marble**

*immobile*, *magical*

A slab of once-beautiful stone, some 30 feet tall and about half again as wide, standing in a vaulted chamber, surrounded by deeply etched runes that speak of union and protection, consumption and sacrifice. The stone bears a multitude of chips, gouges, and cracks. Dust and debris surround it.

When you *pry a chip of stone from the marble and swallow it whole, while standing within the ring of runes*, your flesh becomes like stone and your DEX becomes -2 until the effect ends. The next three times you would suffer physical harm, the marble slab suffers the harm instead. The effect ends after the third blow.

## **Lithic servant**

*Horde*, *construct*, *implacable*

**HP** 11; **Armor** 3 (made of stone)

**Damage** stone fists d6 (*hand*, *grabby*) **Special qualities** externally powered;

bug-like intellect; blind; tremorsense

**Instinct** to follow its last orders

- <sup>ä</sup> Act with eternal patience and endurance
- <sup>ä</sup> Act with no sense of consequence
- <sup>ä</sup> Hurt someone unintentionally while performing its tasks

Imagine one of those artists' mannequins, but full-sized and made of polished rock. Two fingers and a thumb on each hand. Faceless, expressionless, making a stoneon-stone grinding noise as they move.

They're most often found inert and covered in dust, waiting for some energy source like a Stone Lord's song or a thrumming power-crystal—to give them "life." They'll then follow any orders given to them in the Stone Lord's tongue.

A few of these servants are still active, powered by inexhaustible sources in ancient halls. Their joints are worn smooth, their feet and hands ground down to nubs. Some crawl on broken limbs, attempting to complete tasks that will never end.

## **Stone sentinel**

*Solitary*, *large*, *construct*, *implacable* **HP** 24; **Armor** 3 (made of stone) **Damage** crushing fists d10+3 (*hand*, *close*,

*grabby*, *messy*, *forceful*, 3 piercing) **Special qualities** can animate for only a few minutes at a time; dog-like mind; blind; tremorsense

## **Instinct** to protect its ward

- <sup>ä</sup> Emit an ominous hum, a warning as it powers up
- <sup>ä</sup> Move with shocking, unexpected speed (but lose 1d4 HP)

Articulated statues, squat and wide, with huge stone hands, set to watch forever over some ruin, passage, or treasure. Their stone "minds" are full of vigilance, and violence.

Each sentinel bears a power-crystal in its chest, slowly absorbing vibrations through earth and stone. A few days of charging is enough to animate the construct for only a few minutes—which is usually more than enough time for it to repel (or crush) an invader.

## **Mosaic swarm**

*Solitary*, *large*, *construct*, *amorphous*, *stealthy*

**HP** 24; **Armor** 3 (made of thousands of ceramic tiles)

**Damage** jagged fists of ceramic d10+3 (*hand*, *close*, *forceful*)

**Special qualities** externally powered; buglike mind; blind; tremorsense; unharmed by piercing weapons; half damage from cutting weapons; vulnerable to attacks that are blunt and *forceful* or *area*

**Instinct** to destroy intruders

- <sup>ä</sup> Wait for intruders to get close, rattling softly
- <sup>ä</sup> Explode into a cloud of whirling ceramic tiles, d8+1 damage (*area*, *messy*, 1 piercing)
- <sup>ä</sup> Reform into a vaguely humanoid mass

Stone Lords adorned their halls with elaborate tilework and mosaics. Most of them were decorative. Most of them.

**Something interesting**: Either some spirit is possessing these tiles, or this is some sort of construct. If it's a spirit, there must be a tether. If it's a Stone Lord construct, well, the individual tiles are too small to power or direct themselves.

**Something useful**: The mosaic's frame remained on the wall, and it's glowing blue—aetherium runes! Destroy the runes, and the construct should fall apart.

## **Pacifier**

*Group*, *small*, *construct*, *amorphous* **HP** 10; **Armor** 4 (just a stone sphere)

**Damage** stupefying pulse of sound d6 (*reach*, *area*, *reload*, ignores armor)

**Special qualities** must recharge after a few hours; limited autonomy; blind; tremorsense; has trouble with stairs; vulnerable to blunt, forceful attacks

**Instinct** to keep mortals docile

- <sup>ä</sup> Sense and follow strong emotions
- <sup>ä</sup> Hum a soothing, pacifying sound (*near*, *area*)
- <sup>ä</sup> Sound the alarm, call for reinforcements

They're basically hollow stone spheres, maybe scratched and dinged but otherwise featureless. Often found in their little alcoves, charging. The Stone Lords would deploy them among the rowdier servants to keep things running smoothly.

When you *are in range of a pacifier's hum*, your emotions lose their edge. When you *rouse yourself to act violently*, urgently, or passionately, roll +WIS. If you *are in range of multiple pacifiers humming in unison*, you have disadvantage. **On a 10+**, you shake off the effect; **on a 7-9**, you shake off the effect but pick 1.

- Your mind is addled; mark *dazed*
- Your freedom is short-lived; the effect returns if you pause for any reason
- You get carried away or take things too far, resulting in a consequence of the GM's choice
