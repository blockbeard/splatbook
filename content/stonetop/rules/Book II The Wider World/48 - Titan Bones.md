# Titan Bones

[[14 - The Flats#The Flats|126\)]]. A huge, fossilized skeleton is visible on one side of the hill.

The **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|Highway]]** runs past the hill to the east; a wayside sits at its foot. The hill rises over 300' above the prairie. The northern, leeward face is steep, rocky, cliff-like. The other sides are rugged but manageable slopes, covered in sparse, gnarled oaks and smaller brush.

The crown is flat, sparsely wooded, the highest point for nigh on 100 miles. When Stonetop or the **[[26 - Hillfolk#Hillfolk|Hillfolk]]** need to signal each other, they light a great bonfire up there, on the top of Titan Bones.

## **Impressions**

- The hill on the horizon, never seeming to get closer until it's right up on you
- Those bones, bigger than they ought to be, bigger than any beast you've seen
- A sense of smallness, insignificance
- Southerly wind: ceaseless, buffeting
- Riders approaching, the anticipation as you wait

**Tip:** Up top, portray NPCs with short, loud sentences, like they're shouting over the wind. Ask, "What?" if players don't do the same. Down at the wayside, keep looking up and away, like NPCs are distracted by the hill.

## **Questions**

- Which of you has been (t)here before? Why?
- Do the bones peek out of the sheer rock face, or jut from the southern slopes, or lie flat on the crown, or what?
- According to the tales, what are they bones of? How did they come to be here?
- What superstitions surround the bones? What little rites do folks observe in their presence?
- What other oddities have you found on or around the hill?

## **Meeting place**

A footpath leads from the wayside, climbing the rugged slope in a series of switchbacks. It takes half an hour to reach the crown at a reasonable pace, but a fit person could charge straight up in just a few minutes (at considerable risk of injury or mishap).

When the PCs wish to summon the Hillfolk to a meeting, they must:

- Travel to Titan Bones and climb the hill Wait for a clear day, with no rain or haze Spend the best part of a day gathering fuel and stoking a bonfire
- Tend and feed the bonfire all through the night and into the early morn
- Wait for the Hillfolk to arrive

Choose or roll for how long before the Hillfolk arrive, what happens while they wait, and the disposition of the Hillfolk who show up. It's usually a small group of **[[26 - Hillfolk#Hillfolk rider|riders]]** who respond to the signal, maybe with a **[[26 - Hillfolk#Upland horse|spirit-talker]]**.

| 2-3 | 4 to 5 days               |

## **1d6** disposition

- 1 Angry about something, perhaps openly hostile, perhaps trying to hide it
- 2 Blustering, proud, keen to show off and make a point
- 3 Out of sorts, possibly in trouble, maybe looking for help
- 4-5 Reserved, aloof, wondering what this is about, if it's a waste of time
- 6 Curious, eager to trade and share news

Should the Hillfolk light a signal fire themselves, folks in Stonetop will likely see the pillar of smoke first, then the fire as the sun sets. If it burns through the night and into the morning, that means the Hillfolk wish to meet.

Meetings take place on the wayside at the foot of the hill, under the protection of the pavestones' **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|wards against violence]]**. Hillfolk will assume that anything else is a trap.

- How often do meetings like this occur?
- When was the last meeting called? By whom? What was it about?
- With which group of Hillfolk do y'all normally meet?
- How do you and the Hillfolk traditionally greet each other? What other customs do you observe?
- Where exactly do folks typically light a signal fire? What's special about it?
- What else can be found up on the hill's crown?

## **The titan's bones**

The fossilized bones on the hill are clearly ancient, and far bigger than anything has a right to be. But what are they the bones of? That's for you and your group to decide, but perhaps they are the bones of…

- … one of the three great beasts vanquished by **[[49 - Tor, the Rain-maker#Tor, the Rain-maker|Tor]]**;
- … one of the **[[36 - Primordial Powers#**Questions**|First Creators]]**, slain by the **[[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|Things Below]]** as they were imprisoned;
- … some fell beast brewed up by the **[[24 - Green Lords#Names|Green Lords]]**;
- … a mighty **[[43 - Stone Lords#Some examples:|Stone Lord construct]]**, not a creature at all;
- … a colossal **[[46 - The Things Below#**Thrall**|emanation]]** of the Things Below, a world-ending horror, vitrified by **Stormcatcher**'s [[39 - The Ruined Tower#Stormcatcher's tower|Stormcatcher’s tower]] magic;
- … some combination of the above; or
- … something else entirely.

## **Ruins of the past**

Today, Titan Bones is little more than a landmark and a meeting place, but what was it in the past? That's for you to decide, but perhaps it was once…

- … a site sacred to the **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]**, a place of pilgrimage;
- … a fortress or trading post of the **[[43 - Stone Lords#Stone Lords|Stone Lords]]**;
- … one of the **Tempest Lords**' [[45 - Tempest Lords|Tempest Lords]] sky-islands, crashed or landed and (temporarily?) unable to fly;
- … part of **Stormcatcher**'s [[39 - The Ruined Tower#Stormcatcher's tower|Stormcatcher’s tower]] domain: a lookout, a laboratory, a power station, etc.;
- … a fortress from the time of the **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow Builders|Barrow Builders]]**, likely **[[10 - Death and the Undying#**Death's Door**|haunted]]** from the battles fought here;

- … a **[[46 - The Things Below#**1d12** origin|corrupted site]]**, a place of great evil, now mostly cleansed or at least contained;
- … some combination of the above; or
- … something else entirely.

Whatever you decide, consider how that interacts with your decisions about the titan's bones (see previous column). For example, if they are the bones of a great beast and the hill was once a sky-island, did the beast and sky-island bring each other low? Did the beast (or its bones) power the sky-island? Or what?

## **Dangers**

- A nasty turn of the weather
- The **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#**Waysides**|nosgalau]]**, calling out to those on the hill
- The **[[10 - Death and the Undying#**Pyped**|ghosts]]** of those who died here or at nearby battlefields
- A **[[14 - The Flats#**1d12** battlefield or remains|prairie fire]]** lit by an errant cinder
- **[[40 - South Manmarch#**Horned drake**|Bandits]]**, encamped on the hill or drawn by the signal fire
- Something big and dangerous, on the hunt or lairing on the hill, like a **[[14 - The Flats#**Bandit**|beznpol]]**, a **[[14 - The Flats#**Beznpol**|frythanc]]**, or a **[[42 - The Steplands#1d6 natural beast or spirit|kleztigr]]**
- A nest of **[[14 - The Flats#Hlomren|hlomren]]**, infesting the hillside
- Spirits of the wild: a **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#The Brennauwed|brennauwed]]**, a flock of **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Andalau|andalau]]**), or a **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#**HP** 24; **Armor** 0|fadyn]]**
- Local **[[39 - The Ruined Tower#Dangers|blue magpies]]**, some real jerks
- A **rockslide** or **[[42 - The Steplands#1d6 natural beast or spirit|mudslide]]**
- Something inspired or suggested by the true nature of the titan's bones, and/or the ruins of the past
