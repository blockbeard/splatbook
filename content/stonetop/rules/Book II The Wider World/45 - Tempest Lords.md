# Tempest Lords

**Tempest Lords**

They were, perhaps, the first of the **[[30 - The Makers#The Makers|Makers]]**. They charted the seas and the stars. They leashed the elements to their will. They built mighty ships and flying castles, sailed forth from their island home and conquered. All who stood against them were reduced to ash and glass and slag.

The pride of the Tempest Lords was warranted, but their hubris led to their fall. They sought to tear their entire homeland free from the earth, to set it floating above the world they ruled.

They failed. Calamity followed.

The seas boiled and belched forth horrors. Fire rained from the sky. Their island capital sank beneath the waves, and the spine of their empire was shattered. Civil war erupted. Colonies rebelled.

The Tempest Lords who survived were scattered to the winds. Some tried to restore the empire, but met violent ends at the hands of rivals or would-be subjects. Soon, only a few Tempest Lords remained, enigmas even among other Makers.

One of these survivors, Stormcatcher, made her way to the **[[14 - The Flats#The Flats|the Flats]]**, beyond even the most far-flung of the empire's old outposts. There, between four other nations of Makers, she built her tower. And when the horrors of the **[[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|Things Below]]** were set loose upon the world, Stormcatcher's tower—what folks now call the **[[39 - The Ruined Tower#The Ruined Tower|Ruined Tower]]** was the last of the Makers' enclaves to fall.

## **Language and names**

Tempest Lord writing was ideogrammatic (characters represent ideas, not sounds), and they spoke a primordial language—all who heard it understood it as their native tongue. As such, the names of Tempest Lords are rendered as descriptions, titles, or honorifics.

Example names: Brinedrinker, Eyes-Like-Lightning, Fardweller, Foehammer, Lightning-Is-Her-Spear, His-Smile-Isa-Harbor, Mind-Like-Fire, Moonspeaker, Oarbreaker, Rainchaser, Soother-Of-Waves, Sparkspitter, Squalltalker, Starfinder, Taller-Than-Masts, Voice-of-Thunder, Wavecutter, Waymaker, Windwalker

When other Makers spoke or wrote of them, they used their own tongue. Windwalker was called *Vetrodokha* by the **[[24 - Green Lords#Green Lords|Green Lords]]** and *Avaelkerzhaer* by the **[[43 - Stone Lords#Stone Lords|Stone Lords]]**.

## **Making aetherium**

A clever arcanist might rediscover the process of making aetherium. To do so, you must…

- Study a largely-intact aetherium crucible for a few weeks
- Study both active and depleted aetherium for a few weeks
- Consult a knowledgeable metalsmith (or be one yourself )
- Acquire the appropriate chemics (Value 0) and a sizeable mass of copper (Value 1, *immobile*)
- Devise some way to introduce lightning to molten copper
- Risk injury, fire, destruction, and failure

When you *meet all the requirements*, you can transform a purse of coppers (Value 0) and the appropriate chemics (Value 0) into an ingot or casting of pure aetherium (*magical*, *crude*, possibly dangerous, Value 1 to those-who-know). The process is risky; you are Defying Danger each time you undertake it.

To imbue aetherium with one of the characteristics above, you'll need access to a trove of Tempest Lord or Forge Lord lore, at least. Beyond that, **Make a Plan** with the GM.

## Elemental vortex

Picture a filigree sphere of aetherium, maybe 6 inches across, a glowing mote swirling at its center. This device-called a matrix-binds an elemental spirit [[41 - Spirits of the Wild#**Elemental spirits**|Spirits of the Wild]] and absorbs a particular force, with which the spirit is able to manifest a swirling vortex.

A spirit bound to a matrix must follow any orders given in the Tempest Lord's primordial language. As few alive can give them such orders, any matrices encountered will likely be acting on orders from centuries ago.

Power and size: Each matrix absorbs a particular type of power, and current HP of its vortex reflects its current power. As a vortex gains or loses HP, its size, armor, and damage change as well. At 0 HP, it dissipates and its matrix falls to the ground, inert until it absorbs more HP.

Pick or roll to determine a vortex's starting size, HP, Armor, and Damage.
