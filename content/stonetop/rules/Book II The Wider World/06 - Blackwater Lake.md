# Blackwater Lake

In the west of the **[[42 - The Steplands#Spring|Steplands]]**, in the shadow of the **[[27 - Huffel Peaks#Huffel Peaks|Huffel Peaks]]**, there is a large and placid lake. A low ridge surrounds it, and a wide, rocky shore. The Aberzh River flows in from the west. No rivers or streams flow out.

The water is dark, and very deep, and very, very still. It is always cold but never freezes. There are no waves. The wind shuns the surface. No birds dare alight.

This was once great Dulenn, city of Dark Waters, the slow-beating heart of the **Stone Lords**' vast domain [[43 - Stone Lords|Stone Lords]]. Whereas **[[47 - Three Coven Lake#Three Coven Lake|Teirstered]]** was a city of towers and splendor, Dulenn was a solemn place. A quiet warren, carved and sung from bedrock with the beauty of a limestone cave.

There was no lake, then. Not above the ground. But it was called "Dark Waters" for a reason. For seven deep tunnels led to seven mighty seals, each guarded by Stone Lord sentinels who sang a ceaseless lulling song. Behind those doors was a vast and lightless lake. A hole in the earth. A **[[06 - Blackwater Lake#**Call of Daagon**|Mouth of Daagon]]**.

When the **[[24 - Green Lords#Green Lords|Green Lords]]** roused the Things Below to help them quell the **[[12 - Fae#Fae|Fae]]**, the seals below Dulenn cracked. Daagon began to whisper. Began to sing. Resentment grew among the Stone Lords' human servants. Leaders rose among them: cunning, confident, and eloquent, knowing things they oughtn't, awash in uncanny power. Cults of liberation formed. A conspiracy grew.

Seven sorcerers infiltrated Dulenn. They found the tunnels. They slew the sentinels. They broke the seals.

And Daagon, Who Waits in Deep Water, waited no more. It thrashed. It opened its yawning dark wet jaws. Dulenn fell into the earth. And the Dark Water, the Blackwater, surged in to flood the hole.

## **Impressions**

- Striations in nearby outcrops, tilted towards the lake
- The damp, mineral smell of wet stone
- Clouds, stars, shoreline—all reflected in the lake
- Stillness, quiet, echoes of the slightest sound
- At times: thick banks of fog, wet, cloying, isolating

Tip: lower your voice; speak slowly, softly, make them lean in to hear you.

- A dangerous artifact needs to be cast into the Mouth of Daagon.
- Certain NPCs hear a silent song, calling them to Blackwater Lake.

- From there, it will take at least 2 days and corresponding amount of supplies
- You need a knowledgeable guide.

## **Areas**

- **Nearby hills:** A soft place, where paths might change in the fog, full of ruins, **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow|barrow mounds]]**, and **[[42 - The Steplands#Caves|caves]]**. Bedrock and ruins all tilt towards the lake.
- **Outer ridge:** An escarpment that rings the lake, dropping 50-100' to the shores. Lightly wooded up top. Holds a number of ruins, buried at odd angles, some poking out.
- **Shore:** Extends 100-200' from the outer ridge. Lots of rubble, much of it ancient masonry. Thick lichen, grass that leans away from the lake.
- **The Aberzh River:** Cuts through the western ridge, then widens and flows lazily to the lake. Muddy, shallow, rocky. Lots of dark algae.
- **The Pillar:** On the north shore, 40' wide and over 100' tall. Made of salvaged masonry stones, crudely stacked. No obvious entrance. A precarious climb. Feels cursed.
- **The lake:** About 2 x 3 miles. Impossibly still, dark, reflective. Always cold, never freezes. Hundreds of feet deep near the center.

#### **The Blackwater**

 The lake is always frigid but never freezes over. It is weirdly dark and still, smooth like glass. Any disturbance ripples out and out and out. It feels like it is sleeping. Like it's lying in wait.

### Hazards include:

- Instinctive dread, from a generational fear of water
- The fact that they probably can't swim
- Shivering cold, the threat of debilities or even **[[54 - The Whitefang Mountains#**Ice drake**|hypothermia]]**
- Poor footing (slick rocks, mud, algae)
- Steep, unexpected drop-offs
- Getting dragged down by their gear

**Drowning**

 When you *plunge beneath the water*, hold Breath equal to 1 + your CON. If you first took a deep breath, hold 2 extra Breath.

When you *make a move while under the Blackwater*, and that move involves a roll, choose 1 of the following after the move is resolved:

- Spend 1 Breath
- Mark a debility
- Lose yourself in the Blackwater

When you *lose yourself in the Blackwater*, time seems to stop and you find yourself adrift in a watery, hungry void. Roll +WIS: **on a 7+**, you snap back to reality, somehow holding 2 Breath. But also, **on a 10+**, choose 1; on a 7-9, choose 2:

- Gain a mark [[46 - The Things Below#**1d12** origin|The Things Below]] of Daagon; you are now *corrupted*
- Name something you possess that you love or treasure; it's gone
- Choose something about yourself that you value (a move, your instinct, something about your appearance); lose it

**On a 6-**, either embrace Daagon as your master (gain the Thrall insert), or else make one last move before you are swallowed by the **Mouth of Daagon** (next page).

## **Call of Daagon**

**[[46 - The Things Below#**For We Are Many**|Daagon]]** still sings its silent song. It calls out to the weak-willed, and to those who nurse jealousy or greed in their souls. It whispers to them from afar, and compels them towards Blackwater Lake. It urges the weak-willed to enter the water and be devoured. To the jealous and the greedy, it offers power, wealth, or immortality. For a price, of course.

**Mouth of Daagon**

 At the bottom of the lake, hundreds of feet down, is a Mouth of Daagon. Anything dropped into the deep water will make its way there in time. Valuables left on the shore or cast into the shallows will likely be found by the **[[06 - Blackwater Lake#**Servants of Daagon**|servants]]** and delivered to the Mouth on your behalf. Dangerous artifacts left for the servants might be delivered and consumed, or not.

When *the Mouth swallows something*, Daagon consumes it utterly. When *the Mouth swallows someone*, they are gone, body and soul.

When you *feed the Mouth with something or someone beloved* (by anyone, not just you), Daagon rewards you.
