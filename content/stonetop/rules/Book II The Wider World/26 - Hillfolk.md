# Hillfolk

The **[[42 - The Steplands#The Steplands|Steplands]]** are home to dozens of nomadic bands, collectively called the Hillfolk. The bands are tied together into a bewildering tapestry of kinship and intermarriage and treaty, rivalry and blood feud and long-standing grudge.

Some Hillfolk roam the **[[14 - The Flats#The Flats|Flats]]** in warmer months. Some bands trade with Stonetop, at least on occasion, signaling to them at **[[48 - Titan Bones#Titan Bones|Titan Bones]]**. Others trade with **[[32 - Marshedge#Marshedge|Marshedge]]**, and still others with **[[29 - Lygos and the South#**There and back again**|southern merchants]]**. They have little contact with the **[[34 - North Manmarch#North Manmarch|Manmarchers]]**, and tend to treat them with suspicion.

Their wariness towards the Manmarchers is nothing, though, compared to their contempt for **[[22 - Gordin's Delve#Gordin's Delve|Gordin's Delve]]**. The Hillfolk view mining metal ores as sacrilege. They'll gladly steal from the Delvers or loot their corpses, but only the most debased Hillfolk trade peacefully with Gordin's Delve. And only the most shunned and shamed will actually move there and call the mining town home.

## **Lore**

Hillfolk rarely discuss their ancestry with outsiders. They claim an ancient right to the Steplands, but fear the **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow|barrow mounds]]** and many of the **[[43 - Stone Lords#Stone Lords|Stone Lord]]** ruins.

**Something interesting:** The Hillfolk's ancestors were human servants (slaves?) of the Stone Lords. They rebelled, calling on the power of the**[[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|Things Below]]**. Petty warlords and would-be sorcerer-kings—the **[[05 - Barrow Builders#Barrow Builders|Barrow Builders]]**—dominated the Hillfolk for many years after the Stone Lords fell.

**Something useful:** Many Hillfolk traditions stem from their time suffering under the Stone Lords and the Barrow Builders, and the efforts required to throw off the yoke of those rulers. What's one such tradition that you know of, and how can you use it to your advantage?

### **Nomads**

Most Hillfolk bands are nomadic, following food and good grazing for their herds as the seasons change. Each band has traditional territory, including a winter camp with permanent shelter (caves, ruins, stone huts, wooden longhouses, etc.). A number of bands have territory that extends into the Flats or **[[40 - South Manmarch#South Manmarch|South Manmarch]]**, but winter camps are always nestled in the Steplands.

The **[[31 - The Makers' Roads#The Makers' Roads|Makers' Roads]]** are considered communal territory, but otherwise infringing on a band's territory is cause for suspicion or bloodshed. Wise travelers hide their passage, or else bring tribute and actively seek to avoid offence.

# **Hillfolk bands**

Here's a typical band:

**Size** *hamlet* (~25 to 75 souls)

#### **Population** +0

- Trade with (pick 1)...
  - ... Marshedge (rope, textiles, herbs, pottery)
  - ... Stonetop (whisky, potatoes, oats)
  - ... Lygos, etc. (salt, tools)

#### **Resources**

- Horses, sheep, goats, hounds
- Wool, milk
- Forage (fruits, nuts, veggies, honey)
- Hunting (meat, fur, hides)
- Gathering (timber, sap, flint)

#### **Defenses** +0

- Mounted warriors
- Flint-tipped weapons (*crude*)

About a third of each band are able-bodied adults; the rest are children or elders. Maybe half of the adults are riders, and the rest manage life back at camp. Older children typically manage herds, often with the help of hounds. Specialization is rare, and even homemakers and older children are capable fighters.

- Smoke from multiple fires seen over the horizon
- Bleating of sheep or goats, cluck of chickens, bark of dogs
- Thickets and pens for keeping herds
- Stern, suspicious faces and hands hovering near weapons

## . trade opportunities .

If the PCs get a trade opportunity (from the Seasons Change move), a band of Hillfolk might offer the following:

- A good exchange rate, two or even three Value 2 items for 1 Surplus.
- A cartload of timber or a dozen sheep/goats (Value 2), in exchange for help with a problem.
- Horses (Value 3) traded for a similar amount of Surplus (Value 2), plus help with a problem.
- Someone versed in training and caring for horses, willing to move to Stonetop

## **Dangers**

## **Hdour**

 *Solitary*, *cautious*, *devious*, *hoarder*, *magical*, *spirit-wise*

**HP** 14; **Armor** 2 (thick hides, minor wards) **Damage** bronze knife d8 (*hand*) **Instinct** to ignore rules, boundaries, and taboos

- ä Call up a spirit to attend them
- ä Send spirits or beasts to harass, spy, or attack
- ä Reveal some spell or trick prepared in advance
- ä Unleash power they cannot control

**Spirit-talkers** (next page) have great power, but use it with care and only for the benefit of their people. There are many rules that they follow, many things they will refuse to do. Because to step over the line, and to seek power for power's sake, and to use their power selfishly—that is the way of the sorcerer, the hdour.

When the Hillfolk suspect a spirit-talker of sorcery, they exile them. In the worst cases, they bury them alive. Few are willing to deal a death-blow to a hdour, for that's a sure way to get oneself cursed.

Hdour are often in possession of one or more **[[57 - Appendix C - Minor Arcana#An old scroll case , fragile|arcana]]**, which they do not hesitate to use. They are often attended by beasts, **[[41 - Spirits of the Wild#Spirits of the Wild|spirits of the wild]]**, spirits of the **[[10 - Death and the Undying#Death and the undying|dead and undying]]**, and minions of the**[[46 - The Things Below#The Things Below|Things Below]]**.

## **Hillfolk rider**

 *Group*, *stealthy*, *organized*, *horse-wise* **HP** 6; **Armor** 0 to 2 (thick hides, shield) **Damage** flint-tipped spear d8 (*close*, *thrown*, *crude*), cudgel d8 (*close*, *crude*), adz d8 (*hand*, *crude*), or bow d8 (*near*)

**Instinct** to distrust outsiders

- <sup>ä</sup> Ride a steed like an extension of themselves
- <sup>ä</sup> Outmaneuver their prey
- <sup>ä</sup> Take advantage of the terrain

A typical band of Hillfolk has between 6 to 12 riders: adult hunters and warriors. They patrol their territories in twos and threes, seeking game and keeping an eye out for trespassers. The brash and the bold will challenge anyone they don't recognize. More cautious riders will follow unseen and take the outsiders' measure; whether they let them pass, or spook them into leaving, or murder them in their sleep… well that remains to be seen.

When *a Hillfolk rider and their horse attack a foe together*, they deal 1d6+4 damage (the horse's 1d6+3, with +1 for the rider), combining tags from the attacks as makes sense.

## **Spirit-talker**

 *Group*, *cautious*, *cunning*, *magical*,  *spirit-wise*

**HP** 8; **Armor** 0 to 2 (thick hides, shield) **Damage** flint-tipped spear d8 (*close*, *thrown*, *crude*), club or adz d8 (*hand*, *crude*), or bow d8 (*near*)

**Special quality** sensitive to the presence of active spirits

**Instinct** to protect their people

- <sup>ä</sup> Sharply rebuke a spirit or mortal
- <sup>ä</sup> Commune with beasts, or the spirits of a place or thing
- <sup>ä</sup> Take advantage of the terrain
- <sup>ä</sup> Borrow and wield a friendly spirit's power

Now and then, a young Hillfolk shows signs of being touched by the spirits. The signs often come after a sickness or brush with death. They hear voices that aren't there. They find themselves more comfortable with horse and hound than their fellow people. Sometimes, they lose the human tongue altogether and go feral, becoming a **[[42 - The Steplands#**Instinct** to overreact|gouzadn]]**. But when things go right—when they are guided by an elder, and possessed of great self-control they become a spirit-talker for their band.

## **Upland horse**

 *Group*, *large*, *powerful*, *keen-nosed*, *agile*, *brave*

## **HP** 14; **Armor** 0

**Damage** kick, trample d6+3 (*hand*, *close*, *forceful*)

**Instinct** to suffer no fools

- ä Sniff trouble on the wind
- ä Navigate tricky terrain or obstacles
- ä Remain calm, unmoved
- ä Find their own way

Hillfolk bands breed their horses for strength, agility, and courage. The best become steeds for Hillfolk riders. It takes a firm hand and a steady will to handle an Upland, but win one over and you've got a friend for life.
