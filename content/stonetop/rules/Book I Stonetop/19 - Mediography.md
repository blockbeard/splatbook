# Mediography

### **Mediography**

The following works directly inspired *Stonetop*'s mechanics, techniques, setting, themes, and approach to information design.

- *Dungeon World*, Adam Koebel & Sage LaTorra, Sage Kobold Productions, 2012
- *Apocalypse World*, D. Vincent Baker & Meguey Baker, lumpley games, 2010
- *Storming the Wizard's Tower*, D. Vincent Baker, unpublished, 2008
- *Dungeons & Dragons* (4th Edition), Rob Heinsoo, Andy Collins, and James Wyatt; Wizards of the Coast, 2008
- *Blades in the Dark*, John Harper, Evil Hat Productions, 2017
- *Beyond the Wall and Other Adventures*, John Cocking & Peter S. Williams, Flatland Games, 2013
- *The Perilous Wilds*, Jason Lutes, Lampblack & Brimstone, 2016
- *20 Dungeon Starters*, Marshall Miller & Mark Tygart, Lampblack & Brimstone, 2016
- *Trilemma Adventures*, Michael Prescott, Trilemma Adventures, 2019
- *Ars Magica*, Mark Rein-Hagen & Jonathan Tweet, Lion Rampant Games, 1987

### **Blogs**

You can find a lot of writing about *Stonetop* and its development at Jeremy's blog, *Spouting Lore* (2018-2023). Additionally, the following essays and series either influenced techniques and approaches presented in this game or demonstrate the game in action.

- "*Apocalypse World*: Crossing the Line," John Harper, *The Mighty Atom*, 2010
- "Paint the Scene," Jason Cordova, *The Gauntlet Blog*, 2018
- "The 7-3-1 Technique," Jason Cordova, *The Gauntlet Blog*, 2018
- "A 16 HP Dragon," Stras Acimovic, reposted by Sage LaTorra, Latorra.org, 2012
- "Powered by the Apocalypse" (12-part series), D. Vincent Baker, *lumpley games*, 2019-2025
- *A Collection of Unmitigated Pedantry*, Bret Devereaux, 2019-present (especially the "Practical Polytheism" series and the "Iron, How Did They Make It?" series)
- "Xandering the Dungeon," Justin Alexander, *The Alexandrian*, 2010
- "Dungeon Checklist," Arnold K., *Goblin Punch*
- "PTFO: Stonetop" (solo actual-play series), SGH, *PTFO* on Substack, 2022-present

#### caveat emptor

All of the above are relevant and potentially useful as inspiration, but they're not necessarily all good. And some of the creators held and espoused hurtful beliefs, which permeates their work. Others have done or been accused of terrible things. People, like art and our consumption of it, are complicated.
