## **Playbooks**

When you create a PC for *Stonetop*, the first thing you'll do is pick your playbook. Each playbook contains a set of choices, prompts, and options that point towards a specific character archetype, while leaving room to make your character unique.

Your playbook also serves as a character sheet. You'll use it to track your PC's stats, debilities, current HP and XP, moves, and so forth. The playbooks are designed to be printed double-sided and folded down the middle, so that they form a little booklet.

The nine standard playbooks are:

- [[The Blessed]]
- [[The Fox]]
- [[The Heavy]]
- [[The Judge]]
- [[The Lightbearer]]
- [[The Marshal]]
- [[The Ranger]]
- [[The Seeker]]
- [[The Would-be Hero]]

For an explanation of each component of a PC playbook, see **[[03 - Playing the Game#Your character|Your character]]** in the previous chapter.

Each PC also has at least one **insert**. The standard inserts are collected in [[Inserts]]. The village's own playbook is [[The Steading]].

It's a summary of content found elsewhere in this book and *Book II*, and thus is not included in this chapter. In this vault it's embedded in [[The GM]].
> ^the-gm-has-a-playbook-too

When you have questions about particular moves or other elements of a playbook (how they work, what exactly \_\_ means, what constitutes "a creature of darkness," etc.), talk it out with the GM and the other players. Consider what's already been established or implied, and work it out as a group.

Don't be surprised if the GM turns such questions back on you. "Well, what do *you* think constitutes a creature of darkness?" This isn't a trap or a trick! It's an invitation for you to **help author the world**. Answer with whatever seems obvious or interesting to you. Just be prepared for that answer to have lasting implications for how the move works in your game!
